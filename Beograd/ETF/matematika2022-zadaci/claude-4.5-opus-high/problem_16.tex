\section*{Problem 16}
Find the difference between the largest and smallest solutions of the inequality:
\[
\sqrt[4]{(\sqrt{2}+1)^x} + \sqrt[4]{(\sqrt{2}-1)^x} \leq 2\sqrt{2}
\]

(A) 2 \quad (B) 8 \quad (C) 4 \quad (D) 6 \quad (E) 10

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $u = \sqrt[4]{(\sqrt{2}+1)^x} = (\sqrt{2}+1)^{x/4}$.

Note that $(\sqrt{2}+1)(\sqrt{2}-1) = 2 - 1 = 1$, so $\sqrt{2}-1 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}+1}$.

Therefore:
\[
\sqrt[4]{(\sqrt{2}-1)^x} = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}+1}\right)^{x/4} = (\sqrt{2}+1)^{-x/4} = \frac{1}{u}
\]

The inequality becomes:
\[
u + \frac{1}{u} \leq 2\sqrt{2}
\]

\textbf{Analyze $f(u) = u + \frac{1}{u}$:}

For $u > 0$, $f(u) \geq 2$ with minimum at $u = 1$.

Setting $f(u) = 2\sqrt{2}$:
\[
u + \frac{1}{u} = 2\sqrt{2}
\]
\[
u^2 - 2\sqrt{2}u + 1 = 0
\]
\[
u = \frac{2\sqrt{2} \pm \sqrt{8 - 4}}{2} = \frac{2\sqrt{2} \pm 2}{2} = \sqrt{2} \pm 1
\]

So the inequality $u + \frac{1}{u} \leq 2\sqrt{2}$ holds when:
\[
\sqrt{2} - 1 \leq u \leq \sqrt{2} + 1
\]

\textbf{Convert back to $x$:}

$u = (\sqrt{2}+1)^{x/4}$

For $u = \sqrt{2} + 1$:
\[
(\sqrt{2}+1)^{x/4} = \sqrt{2}+1 \implies \frac{x}{4} = 1 \implies x = 4
\]

For $u = \sqrt{2} - 1 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}+1} = (\sqrt{2}+1)^{-1}$:
\[
(\sqrt{2}+1)^{x/4} = (\sqrt{2}+1)^{-1} \implies \frac{x}{4} = -1 \implies x = -4
\]

\textbf{Difference:}
\[
x_{\max} - x_{\min} = 4 - (-4) = 8
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$8$ (option \textbf{B}).
