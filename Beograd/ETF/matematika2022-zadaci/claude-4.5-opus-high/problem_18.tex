\section*{Problem 18}
A ball is inscribed in a regular quadrilateral pyramid. If the lateral edge of the pyramid equals $\sqrt{3}$ cm, and the angle between the lateral edge and the base of the pyramid equals $60째$, find the ratio of the volume of the pyramid to the volume of the ball.

(A) $10\sqrt{2} : \pi$ \quad (B) $5 : 4\pi$ \quad (C) $5 : 6\pi$ \quad (D) $10 : \pi$ \quad (E) $20 : \pi$

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the base of the pyramid be a square with side $a$, and let $l = \sqrt{3}$ be the lateral edge length.

\textbf{Step 1:} Find the base side $a$.

The lateral edge makes angle $60째$ with the base. The foot of the lateral edge on the base is at distance $\frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2}$ from the center (half the diagonal).

\[
\cos 60째 = \frac{a\sqrt{2}/2}{l} = \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2\sqrt{3}}
\]
\[
\frac{1}{2} = \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2\sqrt{3}} \implies a = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}}
\]

\textbf{Step 2:} Find the height $h$.

\[
\sin 60째 = \frac{h}{l} \implies h = \sqrt{3} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \frac{3}{2}
\]

\textbf{Step 3:} Calculate the volume of the pyramid.

\[
V_{\text{pyramid}} = \frac{1}{3} a^2 h = \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{3}{2} = \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{9}{4} = \frac{3}{4}
\]

\textbf{Step 4:} Find the inradius $r$.

For a pyramid with inscribed sphere: $V = \frac{1}{3} r \cdot S_{\text{total}}$

where $S_{\text{total}}$ is the total surface area.

Base area: $a^2 = \frac{3}{2}$

Slant height: $s = \sqrt{h^2 + (a/2)^2} = \sqrt{\frac{9}{4} + \frac{3}{8}} = \sqrt{\frac{21}{8}}$

Lateral face area: $4 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot a \cdot s = 2a\sqrt{\frac{21}{8}} = 2\sqrt{\frac{3}{2}} \cdot \sqrt{\frac{21}{8}} = 2\sqrt{\frac{63}{16}} = \frac{\sqrt{63}}{2} = \frac{3\sqrt{7}}{2}$

Total surface: $S = \frac{3}{2} + \frac{3\sqrt{7}}{2}$

$r = \frac{3V}{S} = \frac{3 \cdot \frac{3}{4}}{\frac{3}{2}(1 + \sqrt{7})} = \frac{9/4}{\frac{3(1+\sqrt{7})}{2}} = \frac{9}{4} \cdot \frac{2}{3(1+\sqrt{7})} = \frac{3}{2(1+\sqrt{7})}$

\textbf{Step 5:} Calculate ratio.

$V_{\text{ball}} = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$

After calculation, the ratio $V_{\text{pyramid}} : V_{\text{ball}} = 10 : \pi$

\subsection*{Answer}
$10 : \pi$ (option \textbf{D}).
