\section*{Problem 19}
Find the largest number of real solutions of the equation $|2 - |x - x^2|| = p$, where $p$ is a real parameter.

(A) 1 \quad (B) 6 \quad (C) 2 \quad (D) 8 \quad (E) 4

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $f(x) = x - x^2 = -\left(x - \frac{1}{2}\right)^2 + \frac{1}{4}$.

This is a downward parabola with maximum $\frac{1}{4}$ at $x = \frac{1}{2}$, and zeros at $x = 0$ and $x = 1$.

\textbf{Analysis of $|f(x)| = |x - x^2|$:}

\begin{itemize}
\item For $0 \leq x \leq 1$: $f(x) \geq 0$, so $|f(x)| = x - x^2$
\item For $x < 0$ or $x > 1$: $f(x) < 0$, so $|f(x)| = x^2 - x$
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Analysis of $g(x) = 2 - |x - x^2|$:}

\begin{itemize}
\item For $0 \leq x \leq 1$: $g(x) = 2 - (x - x^2) = 2 - x + x^2$, minimum at $x = \frac{1}{2}$: $g(\frac{1}{2}) = 2 - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{7}{4}$
\item For $x < 0$ or $x > 1$: $g(x) = 2 - (x^2 - x) = 2 + x - x^2$, maximum at $x = \frac{1}{2}$ (but this is outside the region)
\end{itemize}

As $x \to \pm\infty$, $|x - x^2| \to \infty$, so $g(x) \to -\infty$.

\textbf{Analysis of $|g(x)| = |2 - |x - x^2||$:}

The equation $|g(x)| = p$ means $g(x) = p$ or $g(x) = -p$.

For each horizontal line $y = c$, we count intersections with the graph of $g(x)$.

The function $g(x)$ has:
\begin{itemize}
\item A local minimum of $\frac{7}{4}$ at $x = \frac{1}{2}$
\item Value 2 at $x = 0$ and $x = 1$
\item Decreases to $-\infty$ as $x \to \pm\infty$
\end{itemize}

For the equation $|g(x)| = p$:
\begin{itemize}
\item If $p = 2$: $g(x) = 2$ (at $x = 0, 1$) or $g(x) = -2$ (2 solutions outside $[0,1]$) $\to$ 4 solutions
\item For appropriate $p > 2$: $g(x) = p$ has 0 solutions, $g(x) = -p$ has 2 solutions $\to$ 2 solutions
\item For $\frac{7}{4} < p < 2$: $g(x) = p$ has 2 solutions, $g(x) = -p$ has 2 solutions $\to$ 4 solutions
\item For $0 < p < \frac{7}{4}$: $g(x) = p$ has 4 solutions (2 in $[0,1]$, 2 outside), $g(x) = -p$ has 2 solutions $\to$ 6 solutions
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Maximum number of solutions: 6}

\subsection*{Answer}
$6$ (option \textbf{B}).
