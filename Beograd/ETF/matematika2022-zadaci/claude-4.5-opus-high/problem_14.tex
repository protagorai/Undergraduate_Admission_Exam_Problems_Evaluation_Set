\section*{Problem 14}
Find the number of distinct real solutions of the equation:
\[
\sqrt{1-x} + \sqrt{1+x} - \sqrt{1-x^2} = \frac{5}{4}
\]

(A) 0 \quad (B) 1 \quad (C) 4 \quad (D) 2 \quad (E) 3

\subsection*{Solution}
\textbf{Domain:} $-1 \leq x \leq 1$

Let $u = \sqrt{1-x}$ and $v = \sqrt{1+x}$.

Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item $u^2 = 1-x$ and $v^2 = 1+x$
\item $u^2 + v^2 = 2$
\item $uv = \sqrt{(1-x)(1+x)} = \sqrt{1-x^2}$
\end{itemize}

The equation becomes:
\[
u + v - uv = \frac{5}{4}
\]

Let $s = u + v$ and $p = uv$.

We have:
\begin{align}
s - p &= \frac{5}{4} \\
s^2 - 2p &= 2
\end{align}

From equation (1): $p = s - \frac{5}{4}$

Substitute into equation (2):
\[
s^2 - 2\left(s - \frac{5}{4}\right) = 2
\]
\[
s^2 - 2s + \frac{5}{2} = 2
\]
\[
s^2 - 2s + \frac{1}{2} = 0
\]
\[
s = \frac{2 \pm \sqrt{4 - 2}}{2} = \frac{2 \pm \sqrt{2}}{2} = 1 \pm \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}
\]

\textbf{Check validity:}

Since $u, v \geq 0$ and $u^2 + v^2 = 2$, by Cauchy-Schwarz: $s = u + v \leq \sqrt{2(u^2 + v^2)} = 2$.

Also $s \geq \sqrt{u^2 + v^2} = \sqrt{2}$ (by QM-AM inequality reversed).

$s_1 = 1 + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 1.707 \geq \sqrt{2} \approx 1.414$ \checkmark

$s_2 = 1 - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.293 < \sqrt{2}$ \ding{55}

So only $s = 1 + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ is valid.

For this $s$: $p = s - \frac{5}{4} = 1 + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - \frac{5}{4} = -\frac{1}{4} + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$

$u$ and $v$ are roots of $t^2 - st + p = 0$.

Discriminant: $s^2 - 4p = \left(1 + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)^2 - 4\left(-\frac{1}{4} + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)$
$= 1 + \sqrt{2} + \frac{1}{2} + 1 - 2\sqrt{2} = \frac{5}{2} - \sqrt{2} > 0$

Two positive values of $(u, v)$ exist. From $x = \frac{v^2 - u^2}{2}$, each pair gives one value of $x$.

Since $(u, v)$ and $(v, u)$ give the same $|x|$ but opposite signs, we get 2 distinct solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{D}).
