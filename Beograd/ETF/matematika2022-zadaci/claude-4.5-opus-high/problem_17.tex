\section*{Problem 17}
If the ratio of the binomial coefficients of the fourth and third terms in the expansion of $\left(\sqrt[3]{5} - \sqrt[5]{3}\right)^n$ $(n \in \mathbb{N}, n \geq 3)$ equals 10, find the number of rational terms in this expansion.

(A) 2 \quad (B) 1 \quad (C) 4 \quad (D) 3 \quad (E) greater than 4

\subsection*{Solution}
The general term in the binomial expansion of $(a + b)^n$ is:
\[
T_{k+1} = \binom{n}{k} a^{n-k} b^k
\]

Here $a = \sqrt[3]{5} = 5^{1/3}$ and $b = -\sqrt[5]{3} = -3^{1/5}$.

\textbf{Find $n$:}

The third term has $k = 2$: coefficient $\binom{n}{2}$

The fourth term has $k = 3$: coefficient $\binom{n}{3}$

Given ratio:
\[
\frac{\binom{n}{3}}{\binom{n}{2}} = 10
\]
\[
\frac{n!/(3!(n-3)!)}{n!/(2!(n-2)!)} = \frac{(n-2)!}{3 \cdot (n-3)!} = \frac{n-2}{3} = 10
\]
\[
n - 2 = 30 \implies n = 32
\]

\textbf{Find rational terms:}

The $(k+1)$-th term is:
\[
T_{k+1} = \binom{32}{k} (5^{1/3})^{32-k} (-3^{1/5})^k = \binom{32}{k} (-1)^k \cdot 5^{(32-k)/3} \cdot 3^{k/5}
\]

For this to be rational, both exponents must be integers:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\frac{32-k}{3} \in \mathbb{Z} \implies 32 - k \equiv 0 \pmod{3} \implies k \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$
\item $\frac{k}{5} \in \mathbb{Z} \implies k \equiv 0 \pmod{5}$
\end{itemize}

We need $k \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$ and $k \equiv 0 \pmod{5}$ with $0 \leq k \leq 32$.

Multiples of 5 in $[0, 32]$: $0, 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30$

Check $k \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $k = 0$: $0 \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$ \ding{55}
\item $k = 5$: $5 \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$ \checkmark
\item $k = 10$: $10 \equiv 1 \pmod{3}$ \ding{55}
\item $k = 15$: $15 \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$ \ding{55}
\item $k = 20$: $20 \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$ \checkmark
\item $k = 25$: $25 \equiv 1 \pmod{3}$ \ding{55}
\item $k = 30$: $30 \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$ \ding{55}
\end{itemize}

Valid values: $k = 5$ and $k = 20$

\textbf{Number of rational terms: 2}

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{A}).
