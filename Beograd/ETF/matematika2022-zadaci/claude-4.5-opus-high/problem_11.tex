\section*{Problem 11}
Find the number of distinct real solutions of the equation:
\[
1 + \log_x \frac{4-x}{10} = (\log_{10} x^2 - 1) \log_x 10
\]

(A) 3 \quad (B) 1 \quad (C) 4 \quad (D) 2 \quad (E) 0

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $t = \log_{10} x$, so $\log_x 10 = \frac{1}{t}$ (assuming $x > 0$, $x \neq 1$).

Also, $\log_x \frac{4-x}{10} = \frac{\log_{10}\frac{4-x}{10}}{\log_{10} x} = \frac{\log_{10}(4-x) - 1}{t}$

The equation becomes:
\[
1 + \frac{\log_{10}(4-x) - 1}{t} = \frac{2t - 1}{t}
\]

Multiply both sides by $t$:
\[
t + \log_{10}(4-x) - 1 = 2t - 1
\]

Simplify:
\[
\log_{10}(4-x) = t
\]

Since $t = \log_{10} x$:
\[
\log_{10}(4-x) = \log_{10} x
\]

Therefore:
\[
4 - x = x \implies 2x = 4 \implies x = 2
\]

\textbf{Verify the domain:}
\begin{itemize}
\item $x > 0$: $2 > 0$ \checkmark
\item $x \neq 1$: $2 \neq 1$ \checkmark
\item $4 - x > 0$: $4 - 2 = 2 > 0$ \checkmark
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Verify the solution:}

LHS: $1 + \log_2 \frac{2}{10} = 1 + \log_2 \frac{1}{5} = 1 - \log_2 5$

RHS: $(\log_{10} 4 - 1) \log_2 10 = (2\log_{10} 2 - 1) \cdot \frac{1}{\log_{10} 2}$
$= 2 - \frac{1}{\log_{10} 2} = 2 - \log_2 10$

Let's verify: $1 - \log_2 5 = 2 - \log_2 10 = 2 - \log_2 5 - 1 = 1 - \log_2 5$ \checkmark

There is exactly one solution: $x = 2$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$1$ (option \textbf{B}).
