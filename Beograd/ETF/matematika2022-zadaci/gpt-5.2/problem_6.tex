\section*{Problem 6}
Let $x,y\in\mathbb{C}\setminus\{0\}$ and suppose
\[
\frac{x}{y}+4\frac{y}{x}=2.
\]
Find $\dfrac{x^3}{y^3}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Set $t=\dfrac{x}{y}\neq 0$. Then the condition becomes
\[
t+\frac{4}{t}=2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad t^2-2t+4=0.
\]
Solve:
\[
t=\frac{2\pm\sqrt{4-16}}{2}=1\pm i\sqrt3.
\]
Now compute $t^3$. Write $t$ in polar form: $|t|=\sqrt{1+3}=2$ and
\[
1\pm i\sqrt3=2\left(\cos\frac{\pi}{3}\pm i\sin\frac{\pi}{3}\right)=2e^{\pm i\pi/3}.
\]
Hence
\[
t^3=2^3 e^{\pm i\pi}=8(-1)=-8.
\]
But $t^3=(x/y)^3=x^3/y^3$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$-8$ (option \textbf{A}).
