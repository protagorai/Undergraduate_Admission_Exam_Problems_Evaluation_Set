\section*{Problem 8}
How many distinct natural numbers can be written using digits $1,2,3,4,5$ if each digit may be used at most once?

\subsection*{Solution}
We can form numbers of length $k=1,2,3,4,5$.
For each $k$, the count is the number of permutations of $k$ distinct digits chosen from $5$:
\[
P(5,k)=\frac{5!}{(5-k)!}.
\]
Therefore the total is
\[
\sum_{k=1}^{5}\frac{5!}{(5-k)!}
=\frac{5!}{4!}+\frac{5!}{3!}+\frac{5!}{2!}+\frac{5!}{1!}+\frac{5!}{0!}
=5+20+60+120+120=325.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$325$ (option \textbf{A}).
