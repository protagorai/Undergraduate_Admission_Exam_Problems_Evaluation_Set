\section*{Problem 9}
How many distinct real solutions does the system have?
\[
\begin{cases}
4x^2-5xy+y^2=0,\\
2x^2+y^2-4x-5y+6=0.
\end{cases}
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
The first equation factors via the ratio $t=y/x$ (note $x\neq 0$, because $x=0$ would force $y=0$, which does not satisfy the second equation):
\[
4x^2-5xy+y^2=x^2\left(4-5\frac{y}{x}+\left(\frac{y}{x}\right)^2\right)=0.
\]
So
\[
t^2-5t+4=0 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad (t-1)(t-4)=0,
\]
hence the solutions lie on the lines $y=x$ or $y=4x$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{1) }If $y=x$, substitute into the second equation:
\[
2x^2+x^2-4x-5x+6=0 \;\Longrightarrow\; 3x^2-9x+6=0
\]
\[
\Longrightarrow\; x^2-3x+2=0 \;\Longrightarrow\; x=1 \text{ or } x=2.
\]
Thus $(x,y)=(1,1),(2,2)$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{2) }If $y=4x$, substitute:
\[
2x^2+16x^2-4x-20x+6=0 \;\Longrightarrow\; 18x^2-24x+6=0
\]
\[
\Longrightarrow\; 3x^2-4x+1=0 \;\Longrightarrow\; x=\frac{4\pm 2}{6}\in\left\{1,\frac13\right\}.
\]
Thus $(x,y)=(1,4),\left(\frac13,\frac43\right)$.

\medskip
In total there are $4$ distinct real solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$4$ solutions (option \textbf{B}, ``greater than $3$'').
