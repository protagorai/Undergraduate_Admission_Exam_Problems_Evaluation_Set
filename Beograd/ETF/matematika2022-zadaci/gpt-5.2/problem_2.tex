\section*{Problem 2}
Find the equation of the perpendicular bisector of segment $MN$, where $M(4,2)$ and $N(-2,0)$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Midpoint:
\[
S\left(\frac{4+(-2)}{2},\frac{2+0}{2}\right)=(1,1).
\]
Slope of $MN$:
\[
k_{MN}=\frac{2-0}{4-(-2)}=\frac{2}{6}=\frac13.
\]
Perpendicular slope is $k=-3$. Through $(1,1)$:
\[
y-1=-3(x-1)\quad\Longrightarrow\quad y=-3x+4.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$y=-3x+4$ (option \textbf{C}).
