\section*{Problem 16}
Let
\[
\sqrt[4]{(\sqrt2+1)^x}+\sqrt[4]{(\sqrt2-1)^x}\le 2\sqrt2.
\]
Find the difference between the largest and the smallest solution.

\subsection*{Solution}
Rewrite the fourth roots as powers:
\[
\sqrt[4]{(\sqrt2+1)^x}=(\sqrt2+1)^{x/4},\qquad
\sqrt[4]{(\sqrt2-1)^x}=(\sqrt2-1)^{x/4}.
\]
Note that $(\sqrt2+1)(\sqrt2-1)=1$, hence $\sqrt2-1=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt2+1}$, so
\[
(\sqrt2-1)^{x/4}=(\sqrt2+1)^{-x/4}.
\]
Let $a=(\sqrt2+1)^{x/4}>0$. Then the inequality becomes
\[
a+\frac{1}{a}\le 2\sqrt2.
\]
Multiply by $a>0$:
\[
a^2-2\sqrt2\,a+1\le 0.
\]
The roots are
\[
a=\frac{2\sqrt2\pm\sqrt{8-4}}{2}=\frac{2\sqrt2\pm 2}{2}=\sqrt2\pm 1.
\]
Thus
\[
\sqrt2-1\le a\le \sqrt2+1.
\]
Since $a=(\sqrt2+1)^{x/4}$ and $\sqrt2-1=(\sqrt2+1)^{-1}$, this is
\[
(\sqrt2+1)^{-1}\le (\sqrt2+1)^{x/4}\le (\sqrt2+1)^1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
-1\le \frac{x}{4}\le 1.
\]
Hence $-4\le x\le 4$. The smallest solution is $-4$ and the largest is $4$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$4-(-4)=8$ (option \textbf{B}).
