\section*{Problem 4}
Find all $m\in\mathbb{R}$ such that the quadratic
\[
(1-m)x^2+(m-2)x+1=0
\]
has two distinct real roots.

\subsection*{Solution}
For a quadratic to have two distinct real roots we need:
\[
1-m\neq 0 \quad\text{and}\quad \Delta>0.
\]
Here
\[
\Delta=(m-2)^2-4(1-m)\cdot 1
=\bigl(m^2-4m+4\bigr)-4+4m=m^2.
\]
Thus $\Delta>0 \iff m\neq 0$. Also $1-m\neq 0 \iff m\neq 1$.
Therefore
\[
m\in \mathbb{R}\setminus\{0,1\}.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\mathbb{R}\setminus\{0,1\}$ (option \textbf{D}).
