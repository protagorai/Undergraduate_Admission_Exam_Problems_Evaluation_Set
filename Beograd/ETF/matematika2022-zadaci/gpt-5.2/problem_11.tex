\section*{Problem 11}
Find the number of distinct real solutions of
\[
1+\log_x\frac{4-x}{10}=\left(\log_{10}x^2-1\right)\log_x 10.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
\textbf{Domain:} $x>0$, $x\neq 1$, and $\dfrac{4-x}{10}>0\Rightarrow x<4$.
So $0<x<4$, $x\neq 1$.

\medskip
Let $a=\log_{10}x$ (so $a\neq 0$). Then $\log_x 10=\dfrac{1}{\log_{10}x}=\dfrac{1}{a}$ and
\[
\log_{10}x^2=2\log_{10}x=2a.
\]
Rewrite the equation:
\[
1+\frac{\log_{10}\left(\frac{4-x}{10}\right)}{a}=(2a-1)\cdot\frac{1}{a}
=2-\frac{1}{a}.
\]
Multiply by $a$:
\[
a+\log_{10}\left(\frac{4-x}{10}\right)=2a-1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\log_{10}\left(\frac{4-x}{10}\right)=a-1.
\]
But $a-1=\log_{10}x-1=\log_{10}\left(\frac{x}{10}\right)$, hence
\[
\frac{4-x}{10}=\frac{x}{10}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad 4-x=x\quad\Longrightarrow\quad x=2.
\]
This satisfies the domain, so there is exactly one real solution.

\subsection*{Answer}
$1$ solution (namely $x=2$), option \textbf{B}.
