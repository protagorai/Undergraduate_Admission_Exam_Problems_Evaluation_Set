\section*{Problem 20}
Find the minimal length of the segment between the coordinate axes formed by the tangent line to the ellipse
\[
\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1 \qquad (a\ge b>0)
\]
at a point in the first quadrant.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $(x_0,y_0)$ be the tangency point with $x_0>0$, $y_0>0$ and
\[
\frac{x_0^2}{a^2}+\frac{y_0^2}{b^2}=1.
\]
The tangent line at $(x_0,y_0)$ has equation
\[
\frac{x x_0}{a^2}+\frac{y y_0}{b^2}=1.
\]
Its intercepts are:
\[
\text{$x$-intercept: } y=0 \Rightarrow x=\frac{a^2}{x_0},
\qquad
\text{$y$-intercept: } x=0 \Rightarrow y=\frac{b^2}{y_0}.
\]
So the segment length is
\[
L=\sqrt{\left(\frac{a^2}{x_0}\right)^2+\left(\frac{b^2}{y_0}\right)^2}.
\]
Minimizing $L$ is equivalent to minimizing $L^2$.
Let
\[
u=\frac{a^2}{x_0},\qquad v=\frac{b^2}{y_0},
\]
so $u,v>0$ and $L^2=u^2+v^2$.
The ellipse constraint becomes
\[
\frac{x_0^2}{a^2}+\frac{y_0^2}{b^2}=1
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\frac{a^2}{u^2}+\frac{b^2}{v^2}=1.
\]
Use Lagrange multipliers for minimizing $u^2+v^2$ subject to $\frac{a^2}{u^2}+\frac{b^2}{v^2}=1$.
Set
\[
F(u,v)=u^2+v^2+\lambda\left(\frac{a^2}{u^2}+\frac{b^2}{v^2}-1\right).
\]
Then
\[
\frac{\partial F}{\partial u}=2u-\lambda\frac{2a^2}{u^3}=0 \Rightarrow u^4=\lambda a^2,
\qquad
\frac{\partial F}{\partial v}=2v-\lambda\frac{2b^2}{v^3}=0 \Rightarrow v^4=\lambda b^2.
\]
Hence
\[
\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)^4=\frac{a^2}{b^2}\Rightarrow \frac{u}{v}=\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}},
\quad\text{so}\quad u=v\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}}.
\]
Plug into the constraint:
\[
\frac{a^2}{u^2}+\frac{b^2}{v^2}
=\frac{a^2}{v^2\cdot a/b}+\frac{b^2}{v^2}
=\frac{ab}{v^2}+\frac{b^2}{v^2}
=\frac{b(a+b)}{v^2}=1,
\]
so $v^2=b(a+b)$ and therefore $u^2=a(a+b)$.
Thus the minimum is
\[
L_{\min}^2=u^2+v^2=a(a+b)+b(a+b)=(a+b)^2,
\]
so
\[
L_{\min}=a+b.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$a+b$ (option \textbf{E}).
