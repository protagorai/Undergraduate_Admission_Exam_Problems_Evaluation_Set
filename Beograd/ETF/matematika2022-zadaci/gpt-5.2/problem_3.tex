\section*{Problem 3}
Compute the value of
\[
\left(3^{\frac{2-\log_{3\sqrt3}5+2\log_{9}4}{4}}\right)^6.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Rewrite everything using base $3$.
Since $3\sqrt3=3^{3/2}$,
\[
\log_{3\sqrt3}5=\log_{3^{3/2}}5=\frac{\ln 5}{\frac32\ln 3}=\frac{2}{3}\log_3 5.
\]
Also, $9=3^2$, hence
\[
\log_{9}4=\log_{3^2}4=\frac{\ln 4}{2\ln 3}=\frac12\log_3 4,
\qquad
2\log_9 4=\log_3 4.
\]
Therefore the exponent inside becomes
\[
\frac{2-\frac23\log_3 5+\log_3 4}{4}.
\]
Raising to the $6$th power multiplies the exponent by $6$:
\[
\left(3^{\frac{2-\log_{3\sqrt3}5+2\log_9 4}{4}}\right)^6
=3^{\frac{6}{4}\left(2-\frac23\log_3 5+\log_3 4\right)}
=3^{\frac32\left(2-\frac23\log_3 5+\log_3 4\right)}.
\]
So
\[
=3^{3+\frac32\log_3 4-\log_3 5}
=3^3\cdot \frac{3^{\log_3(4^{3/2})}}{3^{\log_3 5}}
=27\cdot \frac{4^{3/2}}{5}.
\]
Finally, $4^{3/2}=(\sqrt4)^3=2^3=8$, hence
\[
27\cdot\frac{8}{5}=\frac{216}{5}.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\dfrac{216}{5}$ (option \textbf{E}).
