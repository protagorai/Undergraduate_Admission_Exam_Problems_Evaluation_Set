\section*{Problem 10}
Find the minimum value of
\[
f(x)=-\frac{8}{5}x^2+2x+2+\ln(1+4x^2)-\arctan(2x)
\]
on the interval $\left[-\frac{3}{8},1\right]$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Differentiate:
\[
f'(x)=-\frac{16}{5}x+2+\frac{8x}{1+4x^2}-\frac{2}{1+4x^2}
=-\frac{16}{5}x+2+\frac{8x-2}{1+4x^2}.
\]
Set $f'(x)=0$ and multiply by $1+4x^2$:
\[
\left(-\frac{16}{5}x+2\right)(1+4x^2)+(8x-2)=0.
\]
Expand and simplify:
\[
 -\frac{64}{5}x^3+8x^2+\frac{24}{5}x=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
-8x(8x^2-5x-3)=0.
\]
So critical points are $x=0$ and the roots of $8x^2-5x-3=0$:
\[
x=\frac{5\pm 11}{16}\in\left\{1,-\frac{3}{8}\right\}.
\]
Thus we only need to check $x=-\frac{3}{8},0,1$ (endpoints plus the interior critical point).

\medskip
\noindent Evaluate:
\[
f(0)=2.
\]
At $x=1$:
\[
f(1)=-\frac{8}{5}+2+2+\ln 5-\arctan 2>2.
\]
At $x=-\frac{3}{8}$:
\[
f\!\left(-\frac{3}{8}\right)
=-\frac{8}{5}\cdot\frac{9}{64}-\frac{3}{4}+2+\ln\!\left(1+\frac{36}{64}\right)-\arctan\!\left(-\frac{3}{4}\right)
>2.
\]
Therefore the minimum is attained at $x=0$ and equals $2$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{A}).
