\section*{Problem 7}
Find the equation of the circle whose center is the intersection point of the lines
\[
x-2y+4=0,\qquad 3x+y-9=0,
\]
and which is tangent to the line $3x+4y+2=0$.

Options:
(A) $x^2+y^2-4x-6y-2=0$ \quad
(B) $x^2+y^2-4x-6y=0$ \quad
(C) $x^2+y^2-4x-6y-3=0$ \quad
(D) $x^2+y^2-4x-6y+1=0$ \quad
(E) $x^2+y^2-4x-6y-1=0$.

\subsection*{Solution}
The center is the intersection of the two lines. From $3x+y-9=0$ we get $y=9-3x$.
Substitute into $x-2y+4=0$:
\[
x-2(9-3x)+4=0 \ \Rightarrow\ x-18+6x+4=0 \ \Rightarrow\ 7x=14 \ \Rightarrow\ x=2,
\]
so $y=9-3\cdot 2=3$. Hence the center is $C(2,3)$.

The radius equals the distance from $C$ to the tangent line:
\[
r=\frac{|3\cdot 2+4\cdot 3+2|}{\sqrt{3^2+4^2}}=\frac{|20|}{5}=4.
\]
Thus the circle is
\[
(x-2)^2+(y-3)^2=16.
\]
Expanding:
\[
x^2+y^2-4x-6y+(4+9-16)=0 \ \Rightarrow\ x^2+y^2-4x-6y-3=0.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$x^2+y^2-4x-6y-3=0$ (option \textbf{C}).
