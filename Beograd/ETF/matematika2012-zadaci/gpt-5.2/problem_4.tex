\section*{Problem 4}
Solve the equation
\[
2^{16^x}=16^{2^x}.
\]
Options: (A) $\frac12$ \quad (B) $\frac23$ \quad (C) $\frac34$ \quad (D) $\frac45$ \quad (E) $\frac56$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Rewrite $16$ as a power of $2$:
\[
16=2^4.
\]
Then
\[
2^{16^x}=(2^4)^{2^x}=2^{4\cdot 2^x}.
\]
Since the bases are equal and positive,
\[
16^x=4\cdot 2^x.
\]
But $16^x=(2^4)^x=2^{4x}$ and $4\cdot 2^x=2^2\cdot 2^x=2^{x+2}$, hence
\[
2^{4x}=2^{x+2}\ \Rightarrow\ 4x=x+2\ \Rightarrow\ 3x=2\ \Rightarrow\ x=\frac23.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\frac23$ (option \textbf{B}).
