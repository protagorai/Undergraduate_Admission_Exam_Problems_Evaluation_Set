\section*{Problem 10}
If $a\in\mathbb{R}$ and $\left|a+\frac{1}{a}\right|=3$, find $\left|a-\frac{1}{a}\right|$.

Options: (A) $\sqrt5$ \quad (B) $\sqrt3$ \quad (C) $\sqrt2$ \quad (D) $\sqrt7$ \quad (E) $0$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Use the identity
\[
\left(a+\frac{1}{a}\right)^2-\left(a-\frac{1}{a}\right)^2=4.
\]
Since $\left|a+\frac{1}{a}\right|=3$, we have $\left(a+\frac{1}{a}\right)^2=9$, hence
\[
\left(a-\frac{1}{a}\right)^2=9-4=5.
\]
Therefore
\[
\left|a-\frac{1}{a}\right|=\sqrt5.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\sqrt5$ (option \textbf{A}).
