\section*{Problem 13}
If $\log_2 3=a$ and $\log_5 2=b$, compute $\log_{24}50$.

Options:
(A) $\dfrac{b+2}{b(a+3)}$ \quad
(B) $\dfrac{b+1}{b(a+4)}$ \quad
(C) $\dfrac{b-2}{b(a-4)}$ \quad
(D) $\dfrac{b+1}{b(a+3)}$ \quad
(E) $\dfrac{b-2}{(b+1)(a+3)}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Write everything with natural logarithms. From $\log_2 3=a$ we get
\[
\ln 3=a\ln 2.
\]
From $\log_5 2=b$ we get
\[
b=\frac{\ln 2}{\ln 5}\ \Rightarrow\ \ln 5=\frac{\ln 2}{b}.
\]
Now
\[
\ln 24=\ln(3\cdot 2^3)=\ln 3+3\ln 2=(a+3)\ln 2,
\]
and
\[
\ln 50=\ln(2\cdot 5^2)=\ln 2+2\ln 5=\ln 2+2\cdot\frac{\ln 2}{b}
=\ln 2\cdot\frac{b+2}{b}.
\]
Therefore
\[
\log_{24}50=\frac{\ln 50}{\ln 24}
\;=\;\frac{\ln 2\cdot\frac{b+2}{b}}{(a+3)\ln 2}
\;=\;\frac{b+2}{b(a+3)}.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\dfrac{b+2}{b(a+3)}$ (option \textbf{A}).
