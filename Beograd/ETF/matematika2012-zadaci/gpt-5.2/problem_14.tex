\section*{Problem 14}
Two sides of a triangle are $21$ and $9\sqrt2$ with the included angle $45^\circ$. Find the sum of the inradius and circumradius of the triangle.

Options:
(A) $3(2-\sqrt3)$ \quad
(B) $6(\sqrt2-1)$ \quad
(C) $6(\sqrt2+1)$ \quad
(D) $6(2+\sqrt3)$ \quad
(E) $6(\sqrt3-\sqrt2)$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the given sides be $a=21$, $b=9\sqrt2$ and the included angle be $C=45^\circ$.

\paragraph{Third side.}
By the cosine law,
\[
c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos C.
\]
Compute:
\[
a^2=441,\quad b^2=(9\sqrt2)^2=162,\quad 2ab\cos45^\circ=2\cdot 21\cdot 9\sqrt2\cdot\frac{\sqrt2}{2}=378,
\]
so
\[
c^2=441+162-378=225 \ \Rightarrow\ c=15.
\]

\paragraph{Area.}
\[
K=\frac12 ab\sin C=\frac12\cdot 21\cdot 9\sqrt2\cdot\frac{\sqrt2}{2}
=\frac12\cdot 21\cdot 9=\frac{189}{2}.
\]

\paragraph{Inradius.}
The semiperimeter is
\[
s=\frac{a+b+c}{2}=\frac{21+9\sqrt2+15}{2}=18+\frac{9\sqrt2}{2}.
\]
Thus
\[
r=\frac{K}{s}
=\frac{\frac{189}{2}}{18+\frac{9\sqrt2}{2}}
=\frac{189}{36+9\sqrt2}
=\frac{21}{4+\sqrt2}.
\]
Rationalizing:
\[
r=\frac{21(4-\sqrt2)}{(4+\sqrt2)(4-\sqrt2)}=\frac{21(4-\sqrt2)}{14}
=\frac32(4-\sqrt2)=6-\frac{3\sqrt2}{2}.
\]

\paragraph{Circumradius.}
\[
R=\frac{abc}{4K}=\frac{21\cdot 9\sqrt2\cdot 15}{4\cdot \frac{189}{2}}
=\frac{2835\sqrt2}{378}=\frac{105\sqrt2}{14}=\frac{15\sqrt2}{2}.
\]

Therefore
\[
r+R=\left(6-\frac{3\sqrt2}{2}\right)+\frac{15\sqrt2}{2}=6+6\sqrt2=6(\sqrt2+1).
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$6(\sqrt2+1)$ (option \textbf{C}).
