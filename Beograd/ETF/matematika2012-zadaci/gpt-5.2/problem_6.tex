\section*{Problem 6}
Find the coefficient of $x^{27}y^2$ in the expansion of $(x^3+\sqrt{y})^{13}$.

Options: (A) $12$ \quad (B) $1516$ \quad (C) $1312$ \quad (D) $715$ \quad (E) $78$.

\subsection*{Solution}
In the binomial expansion, the general term is
\[
\binom{13}{k}(x^3)^{13-k}(\sqrt{y})^k=\binom{13}{k}x^{3(13-k)}y^{k/2}.
\]
We need
\[
3(13-k)=27 \ \Rightarrow\ 39-3k=27 \ \Rightarrow\ k=4,
\]
and then $y^{k/2}=y^2$ is also satisfied.
Thus the coefficient is
\[
\binom{13}{4}=715.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$715$ (option \textbf{D}).
