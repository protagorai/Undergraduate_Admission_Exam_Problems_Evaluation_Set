\section*{Problem 20}
Find the minimum value of
\[
f(x)=4x+\frac{9\pi^2}{x}+\sin x,\qquad x>0.
\]
Options: (A) $5\pi+2$ \quad (B) $\frac{5\pi}{2}$ \quad (C) $12\pi-1$ \quad (D) $3\pi+1$ \quad (E) $\pi^2$.

\subsection*{Solution}
By AM--GM, for $x>0$,
\[
4x+\frac{9\pi^2}{x}\ge 2\sqrt{4x\cdot \frac{9\pi^2}{x}}=2\sqrt{36\pi^2}=12\pi,
\]
with equality when
\[
4x=\frac{9\pi^2}{x}\ \Rightarrow\ 4x^2=9\pi^2\ \Rightarrow\ x=\frac{3\pi}{2}.
\]
Therefore
\[
f(x)=\left(4x+\frac{9\pi^2}{x}\right)+\sin x \ge 12\pi+\sin x \ge 12\pi-1.
\]
At $x=\frac{3\pi}{2}$ we have equality in AM--GM and $\sin\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right)=-1$, so
\[
f\!\left(\frac{3\pi}{2}\right)=12\pi-1.
\]
Hence the minimum is $12\pi-1$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$12\pi-1$ (option \textbf{C}).
