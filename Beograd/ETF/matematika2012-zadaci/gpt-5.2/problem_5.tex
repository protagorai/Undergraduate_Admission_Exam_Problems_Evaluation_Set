\section*{Problem 5}
If the polynomial $P(x)=x^3+ax^2+bx-4$ $(a,b\in\mathbb{R})$ is divisible by $x^2-1$, find $a^2+b^2$.

Options: (A) $1$ \quad (B) $17$ \quad (C) $5$ \quad (D) $3$ \quad (E) $14$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Since $x^2-1=(x-1)(x+1)$ divides $P(x)$, we have
\[
P(1)=0,\qquad P(-1)=0.
\]
Compute:
\[
P(1)=1+a+b-4=a+b-3=0 \ \Rightarrow\ a+b=3,
\]
\[
P(-1)=-1+a-b-4=a-b-5=0 \ \Rightarrow\ a-b=5.
\]
Solving the system gives $a=4$ and $b=-1$. Hence
\[
a^2+b^2=4^2+(-1)^2=16+1=17.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$17$ (option \textbf{B}).
