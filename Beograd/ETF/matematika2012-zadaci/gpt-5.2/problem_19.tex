\section*{Problem 19}
In how many ways can $5$ boys and $2$ girls be arranged in a row if the girls are not adjacent?

Options: (A) $240$ \quad (B) $3600$ \quad (C) $7680$ \quad (D) $2400$ \quad (E) $250$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Arrange the $5$ boys first: $5!$ ways. This creates $6$ gaps (including the ends) where girls can be placed:
\[
\_\,B\,\_\,B\,\_\,B\,\_\,B\,\_\,B\,\_.
\]
Choose $2$ of these $6$ gaps for the girls: $\binom{6}{2}$ ways, and arrange the two girls: $2!$ ways.
Thus the total is
\[
5!\binom{6}{2}\cdot 2!=120\cdot 15\cdot 2=3600.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$3600$ (option \textbf{B}).
