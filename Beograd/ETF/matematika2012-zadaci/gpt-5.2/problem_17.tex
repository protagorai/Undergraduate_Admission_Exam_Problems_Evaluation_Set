\section*{Problem 17}
Find the number of real solutions in $(0,2\pi)$ of
\[
3\tan^2 x-8\cos^2 x+1=0.
\]
Options: (A) $2$ \quad (B) $3$ \quad (C) $4$ \quad (D) $5$ \quad (E) $6$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $t=\tan x$. Then $\cos^2 x=\dfrac{1}{1+t^2}$.
Substitute into the equation:
\[
3t^2-8\cdot\frac{1}{1+t^2}+1=0.
\]
Multiply by $1+t^2$:
\[
(3t^2+1)(1+t^2)-8=0.
\]
Expand:
\[
3t^4+4t^2+1-8=0 \ \Rightarrow\ 3t^4+4t^2-7=0.
\]
Set $u=t^2\ge 0$:
\[
3u^2+4u-7=0.
\]
Discriminant $D=4^2+4\cdot 3\cdot 7=16+84=100$, so
\[
u=\frac{-4\pm 10}{6}\in\left\{1,\ -\frac{7}{3}\right\}.
\]
Only $u=1$ is valid, hence $t^2=1$ and $\tan x=\pm 1$.

In $(0,2\pi)$, $\tan x=1$ at $x=\frac{\pi}{4},\frac{5\pi}{4}$ and $\tan x=-1$ at $x=\frac{3\pi}{4},\frac{7\pi}{4}$, giving $4$ solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$4$ solutions (option \textbf{C}).
