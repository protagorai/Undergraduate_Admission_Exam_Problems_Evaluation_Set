\section*{Problem 10}
Ako je $a \in \mathbf{R}$ i $\left|a + \frac{1}{a}\right| = 3$, tada je $\left|a - \frac{1}{a}\right|$ jednako:

(A) $\sqrt{5}$ \quad (B) $\sqrt{3}$ \quad (C) $\sqrt{2}$ \quad (D) $\sqrt{7}$ \quad (E) 0 \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $u = a + \frac{1}{a}$ and $v = a - \frac{1}{a}$.

We have:
\[
u^2 = \left(a + \frac{1}{a}\right)^2 = a^2 + 2 + \frac{1}{a^2}
\]
\[
v^2 = \left(a - \frac{1}{a}\right)^2 = a^2 - 2 + \frac{1}{a^2}
\]

Therefore:
\[
u^2 - v^2 = 4
\]

Given $|u| = 3$, we have $u^2 = 9$.

So:
\[
v^2 = u^2 - 4 = 9 - 4 = 5
\]

Therefore:
\[
\left|a - \frac{1}{a}\right| = |v| = \sqrt{5}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\sqrt{5}$ (option \textbf{A}).
