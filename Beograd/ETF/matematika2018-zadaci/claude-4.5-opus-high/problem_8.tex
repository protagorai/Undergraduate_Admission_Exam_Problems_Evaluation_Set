\section*{Problem 8}
Graniƒçna vrednost $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2} \dfrac{\tan\frac{3\pi x}{8} \cdot (\log(5x-9)+1) \cdot (\sqrt[3]{x} - \sqrt[3]{2})}{(x-2) \cdot (\sqrt{2x^3 - x - 5} - \sqrt{2x})}$ jednaka je:

\subsection*{Solution}
As $x \to 2$, this is a $\frac{0}{0}$ indeterminate form. Let's analyze each factor.

\textbf{Numerator analysis:}

$\tan\frac{3\pi x}{8}$ at $x = 2$: $\tan\frac{6\pi}{8} = \tan\frac{3\pi}{4} = -1$

$\log(5x-9) + 1$ at $x = 2$: $\log(10-9) + 1 = \log 1 + 1 = 0 + 1 = 1$

$\sqrt[3]{x} - \sqrt[3]{2} \to 0$ as $x \to 2$

\textbf{Denominator analysis:}

$(x-2) \to 0$

$\sqrt{2x^3 - x - 5}$ at $x = 2$: $\sqrt{16 - 2 - 5} = \sqrt{9} = 3$

$\sqrt{2x}$ at $x = 2$: $\sqrt{4} = 2$

So $\sqrt{2x^3 - x - 5} - \sqrt{2x} \to 3 - 2 = 1 \neq 0$

The limit becomes:
\[
\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(-1) \cdot 1 \cdot (\sqrt[3]{x} - \sqrt[3]{2})}{(x-2) \cdot 1} = -\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{\sqrt[3]{x} - \sqrt[3]{2}}{x - 2}
\]

This is the derivative of $\sqrt[3]{x}$ at $x = 2$:
\[
\frac{d}{dx}\sqrt[3]{x}\bigg|_{x=2} = \frac{1}{3}x^{-2/3}\bigg|_{x=2} = \frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}} = \frac{1}{3 \cdot \sqrt[3]{4}}
\]

Therefore:
\[
\text{Limit} = -\frac{1}{3 \cdot \sqrt[3]{4}} = -\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}} = -\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}}
\]

We can write $2^{2/3} = \sqrt[3]{4}$, and rationalizing:
\[
-\frac{1}{3\sqrt[3]{4}} = -\frac{\sqrt[3]{2}}{3\sqrt[3]{4}\cdot\sqrt[3]{2}} = -\frac{\sqrt[3]{2}}{3 \cdot 2} = -\frac{\sqrt[3]{2}}{6}
\]

Or equivalently: $-\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}} = -\frac{2^{1/3}}{3 \cdot 2} = -\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}}$

This equals $-\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}}$. Note that $2^{2/3} = \sqrt[3]{4} \approx 1.587$, so the answer $\approx -0.21$.

Checking option (B): $-\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}}$

\subsection*{Answer}
$-\dfrac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}}$ (option \textbf{B}).
