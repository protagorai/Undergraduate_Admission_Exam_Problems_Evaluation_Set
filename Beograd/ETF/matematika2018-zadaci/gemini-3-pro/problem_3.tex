\section*{Problem 3}
If for a complex number $z$ the equation $2z + 3\bar{z} = 10 + 3i$ holds, where $\bar{z}$ is the complex conjugate of $z$, then the modulus of $z$ is equal to:

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $z = x + iy$ where $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$. Then $\bar{z} = x - iy$.
Substitute these into the given equation:
\[ 2(x + iy) + 3(x - iy) = 10 + 3i \]
Expand the terms:
\[ 2x + 2iy + 3x - 3iy = 10 + 3i \]
Group real and imaginary parts:
\[ (2x + 3x) + i(2y - 3y) = 10 + 3i \]
\[ 5x - iy = 10 + 3i \]
Equating the real and imaginary parts on both sides:
1. Real part: $5x = 10 \implies x = 2$.
2. Imaginary part: $-y = 3 \implies y = -3$.
Thus, the complex number is $z = 2 - 3i$.
The modulus of $z$ is:
\[ |z| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} = \sqrt{2^2 + (-3)^2} = \sqrt{4 + 9} = \sqrt{13} \]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\sqrt{13}$ (option \textbf{C}).
