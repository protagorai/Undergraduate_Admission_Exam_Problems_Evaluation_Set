\section*{Problem 7}
The set of points in the $xOy$ plane for which the distance to point $A(1,0)$ is twice the distance to point $B(-2,0)$ represents:

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $P(x,y)$ be a point in the set.
The condition is given by $d(P,A) = 2 d(P,B)$.
Using the distance formula:
\[ \sqrt{(x-1)^2 + y^2} = 2 \sqrt{(x+2)^2 + y^2} \]
Square both sides:
\[ (x-1)^2 + y^2 = 4 \left( (x+2)^2 + y^2 \right) \]
Expand the terms:
\[ x^2 - 2x + 1 + y^2 = 4 (x^2 + 4x + 4 + y^2) \]
\[ x^2 - 2x + 1 + y^2 = 4x^2 + 16x + 16 + 4y^2 \]
Rearrange to one side:
\[ 3x^2 + 18x + 3y^2 + 15 = 0 \]
Divide by 3:
\[ x^2 + 6x + y^2 + 5 = 0 \]
Complete the square for $x$:
\[ (x+3)^2 - 9 + y^2 + 5 = 0 \]
\[ (x+3)^2 + y^2 = 4 \]
This is the equation of a circle centered at $(-3, 0)$ with radius $2$.

\subsection*{Answer}
circle (option \textbf{B}).
