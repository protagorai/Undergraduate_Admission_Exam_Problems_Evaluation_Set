\section*{Problem 1}
The value of the expression $\cos^2 2190^\circ + \frac{\sqrt{(-3)^2}}{i^{2020}} + \log_5 \frac{1}{125}$ is equal to:

\subsection*{Solution}
We compute each term separately:
1. $\cos 2190^\circ$:
   Divide $2190$ by $360$:
   $2190 = 6 \cdot 360 + 30$.
   Thus, $\cos 2190^\circ = \cos 30^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.
   Then $\cos^2 2190^\circ = \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{3}{4}$.

2. $\frac{\sqrt{(-3)^2}}{i^{2020}}$:
   $\sqrt{(-3)^2} = \sqrt{9} = 3$.
   For the imaginary unit power, $2020$ is divisible by 4 ($2020 = 4 \cdot 505$), so $i^{2020} = (i^4)^{505} = 1^{505} = 1$.
   The term becomes $\frac{3}{1} = 3$.

3. $\log_5 \frac{1}{125}$:
   Since $125 = 5^3$, we have $\frac{1}{125} = 5^{-3}$.
   $\log_5 5^{-3} = -3$.

Finally, sum the values:
\[ \frac{3}{4} + 3 + (-3) = \frac{3}{4} \]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\frac{3}{4}$ (option \textbf{A}).
