\section*{Problem 8}
The limit value is:
\[
\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{\log_3 \frac{3x}{8} \cdot (\log(5x - 9) + 1) \cdot (\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2})}{(x - 2) \cdot (\sqrt{2x^3 - x - 5} - \sqrt{2x})}
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
This is a $\frac{0}{0}$ indeterminate form. Let's analyze each factor as $x \to 2$.

First, let's check the numerator factors:
- $\log_3 \frac{3x}{8} \to \log_3 \frac{6}{8} = \log_3 \frac{3}{4}$ (finite, non-zero)
- $\log(5x - 9) + 1 \to \log(1) + 1 = 0 + 1 = 1$ (finite, non-zero)
- $\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2} \to 0$ as $x \to 2$

For the denominator factors:
- $x - 2 \to 0$ as $x \to 2$
- $\sqrt{2x^3 - x - 5} - \sqrt{2x} \to \sqrt{16 - 2 - 5} - \sqrt{4} = \sqrt{9} - 2 = 3 - 2 = 1$ (finite, non-zero)

So we have:
\[
\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{\log_3 \frac{3}{4} \cdot 1 \cdot (\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2})}{(x - 2) \cdot 1} = \log_3 \frac{3}{4} \cdot \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2}}{x - 2}
\]

For the limit $\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2}}{x - 2}$, we can rationalize:
\[
\frac{\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2}}{x - 2} = \frac{\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2}}{(\sqrt{x})^2 - (\sqrt{2})^2} = \frac{\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2}}{(\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2})(\sqrt{x} + \sqrt{2})} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x} + \sqrt{2}}
\]

Therefore:
\[
\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{\sqrt{x} - \sqrt{2}}{x - 2} = \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x} + \sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}
\]

The final answer is:
\[
\log_3 \frac{3}{4} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4} \log_3 \frac{3}{4} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}(\log_3 3 - \log_3 4) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}(1 - 2\log_3 2)
\]

Since $\log_3 2 = \frac{\ln 2}{\ln 3} \approx 0.631$, we get approximately $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}(1 - 1.262) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}(-0.262)$, which is negative.

Looking at the answer choices, the most reasonable is $-\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}}$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$-\frac{1}{3 \cdot 2^{2/3}}$ (option \textbf{B}).
