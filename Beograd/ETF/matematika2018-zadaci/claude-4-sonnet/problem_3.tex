\section*{Problem 3}
If the complex number $z$ satisfies $2z + 3\bar{z} = 10 + 3i$, where $z$ is a complex number conjugate to $z$, then the modulus of the complex number conjugate to $z$ is:

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $z = a + bi$ where $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$. Then $\bar{z} = a - bi$.

Substituting into the equation:
\begin{align}
2z + 3\bar{z} &= 10 + 3i\\
2(a + bi) + 3(a - bi) &= 10 + 3i\\
2a + 2bi + 3a - 3bi &= 10 + 3i\\
5a - bi &= 10 + 3i
\end{align}

Comparing real and imaginary parts:
\begin{align}
5a &= 10 \Rightarrow a = 2\\
-b &= 3 \Rightarrow b = -3
\end{align}

Therefore $z = 2 - 3i$ and $\bar{z} = 2 + 3i$.

The modulus of $\bar{z}$ is:
\[
|\bar{z}| = |2 + 3i| = \sqrt{2^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{4 + 9} = \sqrt{13}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\sqrt{13}$ (option \textbf{C}).
