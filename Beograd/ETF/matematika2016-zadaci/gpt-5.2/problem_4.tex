\section*{Problem 4}
Chords $AB$ and $CD$ of a circle are perpendicular and intersect at $M$ such that
$AM=3$, $MB=4$, $CM=2$, $MD=6$ (cm). Find the diameter of the circle.

\subsection*{Solution}
Place $M$ at the origin. Let the chord $AB$ lie on the $x$--axis and $CD$ on the $y$--axis:
\[
A(-3,0),\quad B(4,0),\quad C(0,2),\quad D(0,-6).
\]
The circle has equation
\[
x^2+y^2+ux+vy+w=0.
\]
Substitute $A$ and $B$:
\[
9-3u+w=0,\qquad 16+4u+w=0.
\]
Subtracting gives $7+7u=0$, hence $u=-1$, and then from $16+4u+w=0$ we get $w=-12$.
Now substitute $C$:
\[
4+2v-12=0 \;\Rightarrow\; v=4.
\]
So the circle is
\[
x^2+y^2-x+4y-12=0
\]
or, completing squares,
\[
\left(x-\frac12\right)^2+\left(y+2\right)^2=\frac{65}{4}.
\]
Thus $R^2=\frac{65}{4}$, $R=\frac{\sqrt{65}}{2}$, and the diameter is $2R=\sqrt{65}$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$\sqrt{65}$ (option \textbf{C}).
