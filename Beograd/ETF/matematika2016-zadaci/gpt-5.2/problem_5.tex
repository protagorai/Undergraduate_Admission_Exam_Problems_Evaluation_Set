\section*{Problem 5}
In an increasing arithmetic progression of $11$ terms, the $1$st, $5$th, and $11$th terms form the first three terms of a geometric progression. If the first term is $24$, find the sum of all $11$ terms.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the arithmetic progression be $a, a+d, \dots, a+10d$ with $a=24$ and $d>0$.
Then
\[
T_1=a,\qquad T_5=a+4d,\qquad T_{11}=a+10d.
\]
These three numbers are in geometric progression, so the middle squared equals the product of the ends:
\[
(a+4d)^2=a(a+10d).
\]
Substitute $a=24$:
\[
(24+4d)^2=24(24+10d).
\]
Expand:
\[
576+192d+16d^2=576+240d \;\Rightarrow\; 16d^2-48d=0
\;\Rightarrow\; 16d(d-3)=0.
\]
Since $d>0$, we get $d=3$.
The sum of $11$ terms is
\[
S_{11}=\frac{11}{2}\bigl(2a+10d\bigr)
=\frac{11}{2}\bigl(48+30\bigr)=\frac{11}{2}\cdot 78=429.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$429$ (option \textbf{D}).
