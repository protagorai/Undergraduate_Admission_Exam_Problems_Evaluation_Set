\section*{Problem 2}
Find the number of real solutions of
\[
\bigl|\;|1-|x||-1\bigr|-2=0.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $a=|x|\ge 0$ and $b=|1-a|\ge 0$. The equation becomes
\[
|b-1|-2=0 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad |b-1|=2.
\]
Thus $b-1=\pm 2$, i.e. $b=3$ or $b=-1$. Since $b\ge 0$, we must have $b=3$.
So
\[
|1-a|=3 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad 1-a=\pm 3,
\]
giving $a=4$ (the other value $a=-2$ is impossible).
Hence $|x|=4$, so $x=\pm 4$, i.e. there are $2$ real solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{C}).
