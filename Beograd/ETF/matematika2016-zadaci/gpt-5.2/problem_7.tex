\section*{Problem 7}
Let
\[
f(x)=\ln\!\left(\frac{x^2-1+\sqrt{x^4+1}}{x}\right).
\]
Find $f'(1)$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Set
\[
g(x)=\frac{x^2-1+\sqrt{x^4+1}}{x},
\]
so $f(x)=\ln g(x)$ and $f'(x)=\dfrac{g'(x)}{g(x)}$.

First,
\[
g(1)=\frac{1-1+\sqrt2}{1}=\sqrt2.
\]
Let $N(x)=x^2-1+\sqrt{x^4+1}$, so $g(x)=\dfrac{N(x)}{x}$.
Then
\[
g'(x)=\frac{N'(x)\,x-N(x)}{x^2}.
\]
Compute
\[
N'(x)=2x+\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x^4+1}}\cdot 4x^3
=2x+\frac{2x^3}{\sqrt{x^4+1}}.
\]
Hence
\[
N'(1)=2+\frac{2}{\sqrt2}=2+\sqrt2,\qquad N(1)=\sqrt2,
\]
so
\[
g'(1)=N'(1)-N(1)=(2+\sqrt2)-\sqrt2=2.
\]
Therefore
\[
f'(1)=\frac{g'(1)}{g(1)}=\frac{2}{\sqrt2}=\sqrt2.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\sqrt2$ (option \textbf{D}).
