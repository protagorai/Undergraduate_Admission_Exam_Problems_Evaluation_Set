\section*{Problem 11}
Vertices of a triangle are $A(-8,4)$, $B(-2,1)$, $C(1,-3)$. If the orthocenter is $H(x_0,y_0)$, find $y_0-x_0$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Slope of $BC$:
\[
m_{BC}=\frac{-3-1}{1-(-2)}=\frac{-4}{3}.
\]
Thus the altitude from $A$ has slope $m_A=\frac{3}{4}$, so its equation is
\[
y-4=\frac{3}{4}(x+8)\quad\Rightarrow\quad y=\frac{3}{4}x+10.
\]
Slope of $AC$:
\[
m_{AC}=\frac{-3-4}{1-(-8)}=\frac{-7}{9}.
\]
Thus the altitude from $B$ has slope $m_B=\frac{9}{7}$, so
\[
y-1=\frac{9}{7}(x+2)\quad\Rightarrow\quad y=\frac{9}{7}x+\frac{25}{7}.
\]
Intersect altitudes:
\[
\frac{3}{4}x+10=\frac{9}{7}x+\frac{25}{7}.
\]
Multiply by $28$:
\[
21x+280=36x+100\quad\Rightarrow\quad 15x=180\Rightarrow x_0=12.
\]
Then
\[
y_0=\frac{3}{4}\cdot 12+10=19.
\]
Hence $y_0-x_0=19-12=7$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$7$ (option \textbf{A}).
