\section*{Problem 9}
Let $a\in\mathbb{R}\setminus\{0\}$ and let $x_1,x_2$ be the roots of
\[
ax^2+ax+1=0.
\]
Find all $a$ such that
\[
\frac{(x_1+1)^2+(x_2+1)^2}{(x_1-1)^2+(x_2-1)^2}\le 1.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Divide the quadratic by $a\ne 0$:
\[
x^2+x+\frac{1}{a}=0.
\]
Hence by Vieta,
\[
S=x_1+x_2=-1,\qquad P=x_1x_2=\frac{1}{a}.
\]
Compute
\[
x_1^2+x_2^2=S^2-2P=1-\frac{2}{a}.
\]
Now
\[
(x_1+1)^2+(x_2+1)^2=(x_1^2+x_2^2)+2S+2=\left(1-\frac{2}{a}\right)-2+2=1-\frac{2}{a},
\]
\[
(x_1-1)^2+(x_2-1)^2=(x_1^2+x_2^2)-2S+2=\left(1-\frac{2}{a}\right)+2+2=5-\frac{2}{a}.
\]
Thus the inequality becomes
\[
\frac{1-\frac{2}{a}}{5-\frac{2}{a}}\le 1.
\]
Bring to one side:
\[
\frac{1-\frac{2}{a}}{5-\frac{2}{a}}-1
=\frac{1-\frac{2}{a}-\left(5-\frac{2}{a}\right)}{5-\frac{2}{a}}
=\frac{-4}{5-\frac{2}{a}}\le 0.
\]
Since $-4<0$, this holds exactly when the denominator is positive:
\[
5-\frac{2}{a}>0.
\]
If $a<0$, then $\frac{2}{a}<0$ and the inequality is automatically true.
If $a>0$, we get $5>\frac{2}{a}\iff a>\frac{2}{5}$.
Also $a\ne \frac{2}{5}$ because then the denominator is $0$.

Therefore,
\[
a\in(-\infty,0)\cup\left(\frac{2}{5},+\infty\right).
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$(-\infty,0)\cup\left(\dfrac{2}{5},+\infty\right)$ (option \textbf{B}).
