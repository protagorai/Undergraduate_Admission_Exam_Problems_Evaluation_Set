\section*{Problem 10}
In an isosceles triangle $ABC$ with $AB=AC=b$ and $\angle BAC=30^\circ$, find the sum of its three altitudes.

\subsection*{Solution}
The area is
\[
K=\frac12\cdot AB\cdot AC\cdot \sin 30^\circ
=\frac12\cdot b\cdot b\cdot \frac12=\frac{b^2}{4}.
\]
Let $BC$ be the base. By the law of cosines:
\[
BC^2=b^2+b^2-2b^2\cos 30^\circ
=2b^2\left(1-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\right)=b^2(2-\sqrt3),
\]
so
\[
BC=b\sqrt{2-\sqrt3}=b\cdot\frac{\sqrt6-\sqrt2}{2}.
\]
Altitude from $A$ is
\[
h_a=\frac{2K}{BC}
=\frac{\frac{b^2}{2}}{b(\sqrt6-\sqrt2)/2}
=\frac{b}{\sqrt6-\sqrt2}
=\frac{b(\sqrt6+\sqrt2)}{6-2}
=\frac{b(\sqrt6+\sqrt2)}{4}.
\]
Altitudes from $B$ and $C$ to the equal sides are
\[
h_b=\frac{2K}{AC}=\frac{\frac{b^2}{2}}{b}=\frac{b}{2},\qquad
h_c=\frac{2K}{AB}=\frac{b}{2}.
\]
Therefore,
\[
h_a+h_b+h_c=\frac{b(\sqrt6+\sqrt2)}{4}+b
=\frac{b}{4}\left(4+\sqrt2+\sqrt6\right).
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\dfrac{b}{4}\left(4+\sqrt2+\sqrt6\right)$ (option \textbf{C}).
