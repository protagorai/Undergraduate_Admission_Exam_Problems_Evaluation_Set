\section*{Problem 12}
In the expansion
\[
\left(\sqrt[3]{a}+\frac{1}{\sqrt[6]{a}}\right)^n\qquad (a>0,\ n\in\mathbb{N}),
\]
the sum of the first three binomial coefficients is $121$. Find the term that contains $\dfrac{1}{a}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
The first three binomial coefficients are
\[
\binom{n}{0}+\binom{n}{1}+\binom{n}{2}=1+n+\frac{n(n-1)}{2}=121.
\]
So
\[
\frac{n^2+n}{2}+1=121\quad\Rightarrow\quad n^2+n-240=0.
\]
The discriminant is $1+960=961=31^2$, hence
\[
n=\frac{-1+31}{2}=15.
\]
General term:
\[
T_k=\binom{15}{k}\left(a^{1/3}\right)^{15-k}\left(a^{-1/6}\right)^k
=\binom{15}{k}a^{(15-k)/3-k/6}.
\]
The exponent equals
\[
\frac{15-k}{3}-\frac{k}{6}=5-\frac{k}{2}.
\]
We need $5-\frac{k}{2}=-1$, so $k=12$.
Thus
\[
T_{12}=\binom{15}{12}a^{-1}=\binom{15}{3}\frac{1}{a}=\frac{455}{a}.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\dfrac{455}{a}$ (option \textbf{C}).
