\section*{Problem 18}
Find the sum of all real solutions of
\[
2\sqrt{x}\cdot 4^x + 5\cdot 2^{x+1} + 2\sqrt{x}
= 2^{2x+2} + 5\sqrt{x}\cdot 2^x + 4.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Domain requires $x\ge 0$.
Let $t=2^x>0$. Then $4^x=2^{2x}=t^2$ and $2^{2x+2}=4t^2$, and $5\cdot 2^{x+1}=10t$.
The equation becomes
\[
2\sqrt{x}\,t^2+10t+2\sqrt{x}=4t^2+5\sqrt{x}\,t+4.
\]
Bring all to one side and group:
\[
(2\sqrt{x}-4)t^2+(10-5\sqrt{x})t+(2\sqrt{x}-4)=0.
\]
Factor $\sqrt{x}-2$:
\[
2(\sqrt{x}-2)t^2-5(\sqrt{x}-2)t+2(\sqrt{x}-2)=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
(\sqrt{x}-2)\bigl(2t^2-5t+2\bigr)=0.
\]
So either $\sqrt{x}-2=0$, giving $x=4$, or
\[
2t^2-5t+2=0 \Rightarrow t=\frac{5\pm 3}{4}\in\left\{2,\frac12\right\}.
\]
Thus $2^x=2$ gives $x=1$, while $2^x=\frac12$ gives $x=-1$, which is invalid because $x\ge 0$.
Hence the real solutions are $x=1$ and $x=4$, and their sum is $1+4=5$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$5$ (option \textbf{A}).
