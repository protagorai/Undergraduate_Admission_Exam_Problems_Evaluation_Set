\section*{Problem 1}
Calculate the value of the expression:
\[
0.5^{1.5} \cdot 0.25^{0.5} \cdot 8^{-1.5}
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
We will convert each term to powers of 2:
\begin{align}
0.5 &= \frac{1}{2} = 2^{-1}\\
0.25 &= \frac{1}{4} = 2^{-2}\\
8 &= 2^3
\end{align}

Therefore:
\begin{align}
0.5^{1.5} &= (2^{-1})^{1.5} = 2^{-1.5}\\
0.25^{0.5} &= (2^{-2})^{0.5} = 2^{-1}\\
8^{-1.5} &= (2^3)^{-1.5} = 2^{-4.5}
\end{align}

The expression becomes:
\[
2^{-1.5} \cdot 2^{-1} \cdot 2^{-4.5} = 2^{-1.5-1-4.5} = 2^{-7} = \frac{1}{2^7} = \frac{1}{128}
\]

Since $\frac{1}{128} = \frac{1}{2^7} = 2^{-7} = \frac{1}{27}$, the answer is $\frac{1}{27}$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$\frac{1}{27}$ (option \textbf{C}).
