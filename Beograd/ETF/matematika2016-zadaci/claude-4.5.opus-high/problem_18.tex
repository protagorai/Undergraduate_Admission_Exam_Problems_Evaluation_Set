\section*{Problem 18}
Find the set of all real solutions of the equation:
\[2\sqrt{x} \cdot 4^x + 5 \cdot 2^{x+1} + 2\sqrt{x} = 2^{2x+2} + 5\sqrt{x} \cdot 2^x + 4\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Let's rewrite the equation. Note that $4^x = 2^{2x}$ and $2^{x+1} = 2 \cdot 2^x$.

Left side: $2\sqrt{x} \cdot 2^{2x} + 5 \cdot 2 \cdot 2^x + 2\sqrt{x} = 2\sqrt{x} \cdot 2^{2x} + 10 \cdot 2^x + 2\sqrt{x}$

Right side: $2^{2x+2} + 5\sqrt{x} \cdot 2^x + 4 = 4 \cdot 2^{2x} + 5\sqrt{x} \cdot 2^x + 4$

Let $u = 2^x$ and $v = \sqrt{x}$. Then:
\[2v \cdot u^2 + 10u + 2v = 4u^2 + 5v \cdot u + 4\]

Rearranging:
\[2vu^2 - 4u^2 + 10u - 5vu + 2v - 4 = 0\]
\[2u^2(v - 2) - 5u(v - 2) + 2(v - 2) = 0\]
\[(v - 2)(2u^2 - 5u + 2) = 0\]

\textbf{Case 1:} $v - 2 = 0 \Rightarrow \sqrt{x} = 2 \Rightarrow x = 4$

\textbf{Case 2:} $2u^2 - 5u + 2 = 0$

Using the quadratic formula:
\[u = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 16}}{4} = \frac{5 \pm 3}{4}\]

So $u = 2$ or $u = \frac{1}{2}$.

$2^x = 2 \Rightarrow x = 1$

$2^x = \frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow x = -1$ (but we need $x \geq 0$ for $\sqrt{x}$, so rejected)

\textbf{Solutions:} $x = 1$ and $x = 4$

\textbf{Verification for $x = 1$:}
LHS: $2(1)(4) + 5(2)(2) + 2(1) = 8 + 20 + 2 = 30$
RHS: $16 + 5(1)(2) + 4 = 16 + 10 + 4 = 30$ ✓

\textbf{Verification for $x = 4$:}
LHS: $2(2)(256) + 5(2)(16) + 2(2) = 1024 + 160 + 4 = 1188$
RHS: $4(256) + 5(2)(16) + 4 = 1024 + 160 + 4 = 1188$ ✓

Number of solutions: $2$

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{C}).
