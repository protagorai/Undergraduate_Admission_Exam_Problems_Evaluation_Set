\section*{Problem 5}
In an increasing arithmetic progression of 11 terms, the first, fifth, and eleventh terms form the first three terms of a geometric progression. If the first term of the arithmetic progression is 24, find the sum of all terms of the arithmetic progression.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the arithmetic progression have first term $a_1 = 24$ and common difference $d$.

The terms are:
\begin{itemize}
\item 1st term: $a_1 = 24$
\item 5th term: $a_5 = 24 + 4d$
\item 11th term: $a_{11} = 24 + 10d$
\end{itemize}

These three terms form a geometric progression, so:
\[\frac{a_5}{a_1} = \frac{a_{11}}{a_5}\]

This gives us:
\[a_5^2 = a_1 \cdot a_{11}\]
\[(24 + 4d)^2 = 24(24 + 10d)\]
\[576 + 192d + 16d^2 = 576 + 240d\]
\[16d^2 - 48d = 0\]
\[16d(d - 3) = 0\]

So $d = 0$ or $d = 3$.

Since the arithmetic progression is increasing, we need $d > 0$, so $d = 3$.

The sum of an arithmetic progression with $n$ terms is:
\[S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2a_1 + (n-1)d)\]

For $n = 11$, $a_1 = 24$, $d = 3$:
\[S_{11} = \frac{11}{2}(2 \cdot 24 + 10 \cdot 3) = \frac{11}{2}(48 + 30) = \frac{11}{2} \cdot 78 = 11 \cdot 39 = 429\]

\subsection*{Verification}
The AP is: $24, 27, 30, 33, 36, 39, 42, 45, 48, 51, 54$

The 1st, 5th, and 11th terms are: $24, 36, 54$

Check if GP: $\frac{36}{24} = \frac{3}{2}$ and $\frac{54}{36} = \frac{3}{2}$ \checkmark

Sum: $24 + 27 + 30 + 33 + 36 + 39 + 42 + 45 + 48 + 51 + 54 = 429$ \checkmark

\subsection*{Answer}
$429$ (option \textbf{D}).
