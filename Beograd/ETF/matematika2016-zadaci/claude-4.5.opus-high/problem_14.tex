\section*{Problem 14}
Given a rectangular parallelepiped (box) $ABCDA_1B_1C_1D_1$ with diagonal lengths 7, 8, and 9. The vertices adjacent to vertex $B$ are $A$, $C$, and $B_1$. Find the height of the pyramid $ABCB_1$ from vertex $B$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the dimensions of the box be $a$, $b$, $c$ (length, width, height).

The three face diagonals have lengths:
\begin{align*}
\sqrt{a^2 + b^2} &= 7 \Rightarrow a^2 + b^2 = 49\\
\sqrt{b^2 + c^2} &= 8 \Rightarrow b^2 + c^2 = 64\\
\sqrt{a^2 + c^2} &= 9 \Rightarrow a^2 + c^2 = 81
\end{align*}

Adding all three equations:
\[2(a^2 + b^2 + c^2) = 49 + 64 + 81 = 194\]
\[a^2 + b^2 + c^2 = 97\]

From this:
\begin{align*}
c^2 &= 97 - 49 = 48\\
a^2 &= 97 - 64 = 33\\
b^2 &= 97 - 81 = 16
\end{align*}

So $a = \sqrt{33}$, $b = 4$, $c = 4\sqrt{3}$.

The pyramid $ABCB_1$ has:
\begin{itemize}
\item Base $ACB_1$ - a triangle
\item Apex $B$
\end{itemize}

Place $B$ at origin. Then:
\begin{itemize}
\item $A = (a, 0, 0) = (\sqrt{33}, 0, 0)$
\item $C = (0, b, 0) = (0, 4, 0)$
\item $B_1 = (0, 0, c) = (0, 0, 4\sqrt{3})$
\end{itemize}

The volume of tetrahedron $ABCB_1$ is:
\[V = \frac{1}{6}|(\vec{BA}) \cdot (\vec{BC} \times \vec{BB_1})| = \frac{1}{6}|a \cdot b \cdot c| = \frac{1}{6} \cdot \sqrt{33} \cdot 4 \cdot 4\sqrt{3} = \frac{16\sqrt{99}}{6} = \frac{8\sqrt{99}}{3}\]

Now we need the area of triangle $ACB_1$:
\[\vec{AC} = C - A = (-\sqrt{33}, 4, 0)\]
\[\vec{AB_1} = B_1 - A = (-\sqrt{33}, 0, 4\sqrt{3})\]

\[\vec{AC} \times \vec{AB_1} = \begin{vmatrix} \vec{i} & \vec{j} & \vec{k}\\ -\sqrt{33} & 4 & 0\\ -\sqrt{33} & 0 & 4\sqrt{3} \end{vmatrix}\]
\[= \vec{i}(4 \cdot 4\sqrt{3} - 0) - \vec{j}(-\sqrt{33} \cdot 4\sqrt{3} - 0) + \vec{k}(0 - (-4\sqrt{33}))\]
\[= (16\sqrt{3}, 4\sqrt{99}, 4\sqrt{33})\]

\[|\vec{AC} \times \vec{AB_1}| = \sqrt{768 + 16 \cdot 99 + 16 \cdot 33} = \sqrt{768 + 1584 + 528} = \sqrt{2880} = 12\sqrt{20} = 24\sqrt{5}\]

Area of $\triangle ACB_1 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 24\sqrt{5} = 12\sqrt{5}$

Height from $B$:
\[V = \frac{1}{3} \cdot \text{Area} \cdot h\]
\[\frac{8\sqrt{99}}{3} = \frac{1}{3} \cdot 12\sqrt{5} \cdot h\]
\[h = \frac{8\sqrt{99}}{12\sqrt{5}} = \frac{2\sqrt{99}}{3\sqrt{5}} = \frac{2\sqrt{99}}{3\sqrt{5}} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{2\sqrt{495}}{15} = \frac{2 \cdot 3\sqrt{55}}{15} = \frac{2\sqrt{55}}{5}\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\dfrac{2\sqrt{55}}{5}$ (option \textbf{C}).
