\section*{Problem 16}
The number of real solutions to the system $2^x 4^y = 32$, $\log(x - y)^2 - 2\log 2 = 0$ is:

A) $2$; B) $0$; C) $1$; D) $3$; E) $4$; N) Ne znam.

\subsection*{Solution}
From the first equation: $2^x \cdot 4^y = 32$
Since $4^y = (2^2)^y = 2^{2y}$ and $32 = 2^5$:
\[
2^x \cdot 2^{2y} = 2^5
\]
\[
2^{x + 2y} = 2^5
\]
\[
x + 2y = 5 \quad \text{...(1)}
\]

From the second equation: $\log(x - y)^2 - 2\log 2 = 0$
\[
\log(x - y)^2 = 2\log 2 = \log 2^2 = \log 4
\]
\[
(x - y)^2 = 4
\]
\[
|x - y| = 2
\]

This gives us two cases:
- $x - y = 2$ ...(2a)
- $x - y = -2$ ...(2b)

\textbf{Case 1:} Using equations (1) and (2a):
$x + 2y = 5$ and $x - y = 2$

From the second equation: $x = y + 2$
Substituting into the first: $(y + 2) + 2y = 5$
$3y + 2 = 5$
$y = 1$, $x = 3$

Check: $x - y = 3 - 1 = 2 > 0$ ✓ (so $(x-y)^2$ is well-defined)

\textbf{Case 2:} Using equations (1) and (2b):
$x + 2y = 5$ and $x - y = -2$

From the second equation: $x = y - 2$
Substituting into the first: $(y - 2) + 2y = 5$
$3y - 2 = 5$
$y = \frac{7}{3}$, $x = \frac{7}{3} - 2 = \frac{1}{3}$

Check: $x - y = \frac{1}{3} - \frac{7}{3} = -2 \neq 0$ ✓ (so $(x-y)^2$ is well-defined)

Both solutions are valid, so there are 2 real solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{A}).
