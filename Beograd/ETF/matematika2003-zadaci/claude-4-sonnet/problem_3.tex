\section*{Problem 3}
The value of the expression $\left(-\frac{x-9}{x+3\sqrt{x}+9} \cdot \frac{x^{0.5}+3}{x^{1.5}-27}\right)^{0.5} - x^{0.5}$ for $x \in (0, +\infty)$ is:

A) $x$; B) $3 - 2\sqrt{x}$; C) $-3$; D) $\frac{27}{x}$; E) $\sqrt{x}$; N) Ne znam.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $y = \sqrt{x}$, so $x = y^2$, $x^{0.5} = y$, and $x^{1.5} = y^3$.

The expression becomes:
\[
\left(-\frac{y^2-9}{y^2+3y+9} \cdot \frac{y+3}{y^3-27}\right)^{0.5} - y
\]

We can factor:
- $y^2 - 9 = (y-3)(y+3)$
- $y^2 + 3y + 9$ cannot be factored further over reals
- $y^3 - 27 = (y-3)(y^2+3y+9)$

So:
\[
-\frac{y^2-9}{y^2+3y+9} \cdot \frac{y+3}{y^3-27} = -\frac{(y-3)(y+3)}{y^2+3y+9} \cdot \frac{y+3}{(y-3)(y^2+3y+9)}
\]

\[
= -\frac{(y+3)^2}{(y^2+3y+9)^2}
\]

Therefore:
\[
\left(-\frac{(y+3)^2}{(y^2+3y+9)^2}\right)^{0.5} = \frac{y+3}{y^2+3y+9} \cdot i
\]

Since we need a real result and $y > 0$, we have $y + 3 > 0$ and $y^2 + 3y + 9 > 0$.

Actually, let me reconsider. The expression under the square root must be positive. Since we have a negative sign, we need:
\[
\frac{(y+3)^2}{(y^2+3y+9)^2} \text{ is positive}
\]

So:
\[
\left(-\frac{(y+3)^2}{(y^2+3y+9)^2}\right)^{0.5} = i\frac{y+3}{y^2+3y+9}
\]

This suggests the expression is complex, but let me check if there's a simpler approach.

Actually, looking at the answer choices, let me try $x = 9$ (so $y = 3$):
The expression becomes $0^{0.5} - 3 = -3$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$-3$ (option \textbf{C}).
