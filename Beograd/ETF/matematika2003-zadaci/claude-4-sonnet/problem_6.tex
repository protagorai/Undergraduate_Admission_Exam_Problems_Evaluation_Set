\section*{Problem 6}
The area of a triangle whose angles are $\alpha$, $\beta$, and $\gamma$, and $R$ is the circumradius of the inscribed circle, equals:

A) $2R^2 \sin \alpha \sin \beta \sin \gamma$; B) $\frac{1}{2}R^2 \sin \alpha \sin \beta \sin \gamma$; C) $\frac{1}{2}R^2 \cos \alpha \cos \beta \cos \gamma$; D) $R^2 \sin \alpha \cos \beta \cos \gamma$; E) $2R^2 \cos \alpha \sin(\beta + \gamma)$; N) Ne znam.

\subsection*{Solution}
For a triangle with circumradius $R$, we know that the sides are related to the angles by:
\[
a = 2R \sin \alpha, \quad b = 2R \sin \beta, \quad c = 2R \sin \gamma
\]

The area of the triangle can be calculated using:
\[
S = \frac{1}{2}bc \sin \alpha = \frac{1}{2}ac \sin \beta = \frac{1}{2}ab \sin \gamma
\]

Using the first formula:
\[
S = \frac{1}{2}bc \sin \alpha = \frac{1}{2} \cdot (2R \sin \beta) \cdot (2R \sin \gamma) \cdot \sin \alpha
\]

\[
= \frac{1}{2} \cdot 4R^2 \sin \beta \sin \gamma \sin \alpha = 2R^2 \sin \alpha \sin \beta \sin \gamma
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$2R^2 \sin \alpha \sin \beta \sin \gamma$ (option \textbf{A}).
