\section*{Problem 15}
Poluprečnik kruga koji sadrži tačke $(-2, 0)$ i $(1, -3)$ a centar mu pripada pravoj $x + y = 0$, jeste:
\begin{itemize}
    \item[A)] $\sqrt{13}$
    \item[B)] $\frac{\sqrt{13}}{2}$
    \item[C)] $\sqrt{\frac{13}{2}}$
    \item[D)] $\frac{13}{2}$
    \item[E)] $\sqrt{\frac{13}{6}}$
    \item[N)] Ne znam
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the center of the circle be $C(p, q)$. Since the center lies on the line $x+y=0$, we have $q = -p$. So $C(p, -p)$.
The distance from the center to the given points must be equal (the radius $R$).
Points: $A(-2, 0)$ and $B(1, -3)$.
\[ CA^2 = CB^2 \]
\[ (p - (-2))^2 + (-p - 0)^2 = (p - 1)^2 + (-p - (-3))^2 \]
\[ (p+2)^2 + p^2 = (p-1)^2 + (3-p)^2 \]
Expand the terms:
\[ p^2 + 4p + 4 + p^2 = p^2 - 2p + 1 + 9 - 6p + p^2 \]
\[ 2p^2 + 4p + 4 = 2p^2 - 8p + 10 \]
Subtract $2p^2$ from both sides:
\[ 4p + 4 = -8p + 10 \]
\[ 12p = 6 \implies p = \frac{1}{2}. \]
The center is $(1/2, -1/2)$.
Now calculate $R^2$:
\[ R^2 = \left(\frac{1}{2} + 2\right)^2 + \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{5}{2}\right)^2 + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{25}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{26}{4} = \frac{13}{2}. \]
The radius is:
\[ R = \sqrt{\frac{13}{2}}. \]

\subsection*{Answer}
The radius is $\sqrt{\frac{13}{2}}$, which corresponds to option \textbf{C}.
