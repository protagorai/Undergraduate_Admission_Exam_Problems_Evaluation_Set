\section*{Problem 9}
The perimeter of a triangle whose sides form an arithmetic progression with common difference 4 cm and whose area is 120° is:

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the sides of the triangle be $a-4$, $a$, and $a+4$ cm, where $a$ is the middle term.

The perimeter is $(a-4) + a + (a+4) = 3a$ cm.

For the area, we use Heron's formula. The semi-perimeter is:
\[
s = \frac{3a}{2}
\]

The area is:
\[
A = \sqrt{s(s-a_1)(s-a_2)(s-a_3)}
\]
where $a_1 = a-4$, $a_2 = a$, $a_3 = a+4$.

\[
s - a_1 = \frac{3a}{2} - (a-4) = \frac{3a - 2a + 8}{2} = \frac{a + 8}{2}
\]
\[
s - a_2 = \frac{3a}{2} - a = \frac{a}{2}
\]
\[
s - a_3 = \frac{3a}{2} - (a+4) = \frac{3a - 2a - 8}{2} = \frac{a - 8}{2}
\]

Therefore:
\[
A = \sqrt{\frac{3a}{2} \cdot \frac{a + 8}{2} \cdot \frac{a}{2} \cdot \frac{a - 8}{2}} = \sqrt{\frac{3a^2(a^2 - 64)}{16}} = \frac{a\sqrt{3(a^2 - 64)}}{4}
\]
