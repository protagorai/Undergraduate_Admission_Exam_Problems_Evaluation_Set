\section*{Problem 9}
The perimeter of a triangle whose sides form an arithmetic progression with common difference 4 cm and whose area is 120° equals:

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the sides of the triangle be $a-4$, $a$, and $a+4$ cm, where $a > 4$ to ensure all sides are positive.

The perimeter is: $P = (a-4) + a + (a+4) = 3a$

The semi-perimeter is: $s = \frac{3a}{2}$

Using Heron's formula for the area:
\[
\text{Area} = \sqrt{s(s-a_1)(s-a_2)(s-a_3)}
\]

where $a_1 = a-4$, $a_2 = a$, $a_3 = a+4$.

\[
s - a_1 = \frac{3a}{2} - (a-4) = \frac{3a - 2a + 8}{2} = \frac{a + 8}{2}
\]

\[
s - a_2 = \frac{3a}{2} - a = \frac{a}{2}
\]

\[
s - a_3 = \frac{3a}{2} - (a+4) = \frac{3a - 2a - 8}{2} = \frac{a - 8}{2}
\]

Therefore:
\[
\text{Area} = \sqrt{\frac{3a}{2} \cdot \frac{a+8}{2} \cdot \frac{a}{2} \cdot \frac{a-8}{2}}
\]

\[
= \sqrt{\frac{3a^2(a+8)(a-8)}{16}} = \sqrt{\frac{3a^2(a^2-64)}{16}} = \frac{a\sqrt{3(a^2-64)}}{4}
\]

Given that the area is 120 cm² (I assume "120°" is a typo and should be 120 cm²):

\[
\frac{a\sqrt{3(a^2-64)}}{4} = 120
\]

\[
a\sqrt{3(a^2-64)} = 480
\]

\[
a^2 \cdot 3(a^2-64) = 480^2 = 230400
\]

\[
3a^2(a^2-64) = 230400
\]

\[
a^2(a^2-64) = 76800
\]

\[
a^4 - 64a^2 - 76800 = 0
\]

Let $u = a^2$:
\[

\]

Using the quadratic formula:
\[

\]

\[
\sqrt{311296} = \sqrt{16 \cdot 19456} = 4\sqrt{19456} = 4 \cdot 139.48... \approx 557.9
