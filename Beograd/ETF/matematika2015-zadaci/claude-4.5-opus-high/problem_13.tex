\section*{Problem 13}
Ako je $N$ broj šestocifrenih brojeva koji u svom zapisu sadrže cifru 1 bar na jednom mestu, tada $N$ pripada intervalu:

\subsection*{Solution}
Total six-digit numbers: from 100000 to 999999, which is $9 \times 10^5 = 900000$ numbers.

We'll count six-digit numbers that do NOT contain the digit 1.

For a six-digit number without the digit 1:
\begin{itemize}
\item First digit: can be 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 (8 choices, not 0 or 1)
\item Remaining 5 digits: can be 0, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 (9 choices each)
\end{itemize}

Numbers without digit 1: $8 \times 9^5 = 8 \times 59049 = 472392$

Numbers with at least one digit 1:
\[
N = 900000 - 472392 = 427608
\]

Now check which interval contains $N = 427608$:
\begin{itemize}
\item (A) $[10^5, 2 \cdot 10^5) = [100000, 200000)$ — No
\item (B) $[2 \cdot 10^5, 3 \cdot 10^5) = [200000, 300000)$ — No
\item (C) $[3 \cdot 10^5, 4 \cdot 10^5) = [300000, 400000)$ — No
\item (D) $[4 \cdot 10^5, 5 \cdot 10^5) = [400000, 500000)$ — Yes!
\item (E) $[5 \cdot 10^5, 6 \cdot 10^5) = [500000, 600000)$ — No
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Answer}
$[4 \cdot 10^5, 5 \cdot 10^5)$ (option \textbf{D}).
