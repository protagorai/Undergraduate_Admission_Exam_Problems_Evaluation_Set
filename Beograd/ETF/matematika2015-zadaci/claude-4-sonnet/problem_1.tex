\section*{Problem 1}
If $k \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $0.0010101 \cdot 10^k > 1001$, what is the smallest possible value for $k$?

\subsection*{Solution}
We need to find the smallest integer $k$ such that:
\[0.0010101 \cdot 10^k > 1001\]

First, let's convert $0.0010101$ to a fraction:
\[0.0010101 = \frac{10101}{10^7} = \frac{10101}{10000000}\]

So our inequality becomes:
\[\frac{10101}{10000000} \cdot 10^k > 1001\]

Simplifying:
\[\frac{10101 \cdot 10^k}{10^7} > 1001\]
\[10101 \cdot 10^{k-7} > 1001\]
\[10^{k-7} > \frac{1001}{10101} = \frac{1001}{10101}\]

Since $\frac{1001}{10101} = \frac{1001}{10101} \approx 0.099$, we need:
\[10^{k-7} > 0.099\]

Since $10^{-1} = 0.1 > 0.099$, we need $k-7 \geq -1$, so $k \geq 6$.

Let's verify: For $k = 6$:
\[0.0010101 \cdot 10^6 = 1010.1 > 1001 \checkmark\]

For $k = 5$:
\[0.0010101 \cdot 10^5 = 101.01 < 1001\]

Therefore, the smallest value is $k = 6$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$6$ (option \textbf{D}).
