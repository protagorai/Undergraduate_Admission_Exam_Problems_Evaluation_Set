\section*{Problem 5}
If the surface areas of the faces of a rectangular parallelepiped (box) are 12 cm², 8 cm², and 6 cm², what is its volume?

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the dimensions of the box be $a$, $b$, and $c$. The three different face areas are:
\begin{align}
ab &= 12 \\
bc &= 8 \\
ac &= 6
\end{align}

To find the volume $V = abc$, we multiply all three equations:
\[
(ab)(bc)(ac) = 12 \times 8 \times 6 = 576
\]

This gives us:
\[
a^2b^2c^2 = 576
\]

Therefore:
\[
abc = \sqrt{576} = 24
\]

So the volume is 24 cm³.

\subsection*{Answer}
24 cm³ (option \textbf{A}).
