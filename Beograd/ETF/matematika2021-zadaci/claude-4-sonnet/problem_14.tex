\section*{Problem 14}
In how many ways can two people, $A$ and $B$, share 8 different books such that neither person gets all the books (i.e., both $A$ and $B$ must get at least one book)?

\subsection*{Solution}
Each book can be given to either person $A$ or person $B$. Without restrictions, there are $2^8 = 256$ ways to distribute the books.

However, we need to exclude the cases where:
1. Person $A$ gets all books (person $B$ gets none): 1 way
2. Person $B$ gets all books (person $A$ gets none): 1 way

Using the principle of inclusion-exclusion:
\[
\text{Valid distributions} = \text{Total distributions} - \text{Invalid distributions}
\]
\[
= 2^8 - 2 = 256 - 2 = 254
\]

Alternatively, we can think of it as: person $A$ can get any non-empty proper subset of the 8 books, and person $B$ gets the remaining books.

The number of non-empty proper subsets of a set with 8 elements is:
\[
2^8 - 2 = 254
\]
(Total subsets minus the empty set and the full set)
