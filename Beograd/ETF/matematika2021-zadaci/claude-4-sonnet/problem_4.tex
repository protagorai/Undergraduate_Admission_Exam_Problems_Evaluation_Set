\section*{Problem 4}
Find the number of terms in the expansion of $(a + b + c)^{10}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
The expansion of $(a + b + c)^{10}$ consists of terms of the form:
\[
\frac{10!}{i!j!k!} a^i b^j c^k
\]
where $i + j + k = 10$ and $i, j, k \geq 0$.

This is equivalent to finding the number of non-negative integer solutions to:
\[
i + j + k = 10
\]

Using the stars and bars method, the number of ways to distribute $n$ identical objects into $r$ distinct groups is:
\[
\binom{n + r - 1}{r - 1} = \binom{n + r - 1}{n}
\]

In our case, $n = 10$ (the exponent) and $r = 3$ (three variables: $a$, $b$, $c$), so:
\[
\binom{10 + 3 - 1}{3 - 1} = \binom{12}{2} = \frac{12!}{2! \cdot 10!} = \frac{12 \times 11}{2 \times 1} = 66
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
66 (option \textbf{D}).
