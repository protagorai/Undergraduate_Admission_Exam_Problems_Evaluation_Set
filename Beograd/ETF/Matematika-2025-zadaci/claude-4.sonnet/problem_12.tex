\section*{Problem 12}
The area of triangle $\triangle ABC$ is $30 \text{ cm}^2$. Point $D$ is on side $AB$ such that $AB$ is divided in the ratio $2:3$, and point $E$ is on side $BC$ such that $ED$ is $9 \text{ cm}$ long and normal to side $BC$. Find the length of $BC$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let's set up a coordinate system. Place $B$ at the origin, and let $C$ be at $(c, 0)$ on the positive $x$-axis.

Since $D$ divides $AB$ in the ratio $2:3$, we have:
\[
\overrightarrow{BD} = \frac{2}{5}\overrightarrow{BA}
\]

Let $A$ be at coordinates $(a_x, a_y)$. Then:
\[
D = B + \frac{2}{5}(A - B) = \frac{2}{5}A = \left(\frac{2a_x}{5}, \frac{2a_y}{5}\right)
\]

Since $E$ is on side $BC$ and $BC$ lies along the $x$-axis from $(0,0)$ to $(c,0)$, point $E$ has coordinates $(e, 0)$ for some $0 \leq e \leq c$.

Since $ED$ is normal to $BC$ (which lies along the $x$-axis), $ED$ must be vertical. This means $D$ and $E$ have the same $x$-coordinate:
\[
\frac{2a_x}{5} = e
\]

The length of $ED$ is given as $9$ cm:
\[
|ED| = \left|\frac{2a_y}{5} - 0\right| = \frac{2|a_y|}{5} = 9
\]

Therefore: $|a_y| = \frac{45}{2}$

The area of triangle $ABC$ is:
\[
\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \text{base} \cdot \text{height} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot |BC| \cdot |a_y| = \frac{1}{2} \cdot c \cdot \frac{45}{2} = \frac{45c}{4}
\]

\[
\frac{45c}{4} = 30
\]
\[
45c = 120
\]
\[

\textbf{Case 2:} $BD:DA = 3:2$
\[
D = \frac{3}{5}A = \left(\frac{3a_x}{5}, \frac{3a_y}{5}\right)
\]

The $x$-coordinate of $E$ is $e = \frac{3a_x}{5}$.

The length of $ED$:
\[
|ED| = \frac{3|a_y|}{5} = 9 \Rightarrow |a_y| = 15
\]

The area:
\[
\frac{1}{2} \cdot c \cdot 15 = 30 \Rightarrow c = 4
\]

So $BC = 4$ cm.

\subsection*{Answer}
$4$ cm (option \textbf{B}).
