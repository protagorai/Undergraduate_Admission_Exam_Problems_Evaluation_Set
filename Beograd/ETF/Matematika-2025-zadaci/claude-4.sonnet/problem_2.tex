\section*{Problem 2}
If the surface area of a sphere is $S \text{ cm}^2$ and its volume is $S \text{ cm}^3$, find $S$.

\subsection*{Solution}
For a sphere with radius $r$:
- Surface area: $A = 4\pi r^2$
- Volume: $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$

We're given that the numerical values are equal: $A = V = S$.

So we have:
\[
4\pi r^2 = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3
\]

Dividing both sides by $4\pi r^2$ (assuming $r \neq 0$):
\[
1 = \frac{1}{3}r
\]

Therefore: $r = 3$ cm.

Now we can find $S$:
\[
S = 4\pi r^2 = 4\pi \cdot 3^2 = 4\pi \cdot 9 = 36\pi
\]

Let's verify: 
- Surface area: $4\pi \cdot 3^2 = 36\pi$ cm²
- Volume: $\frac{4}{3}\pi \cdot 3^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi \cdot 27 = 36\pi$ cm³

Indeed, both equal $36\pi$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$36\pi$ (option \textbf{B}).
