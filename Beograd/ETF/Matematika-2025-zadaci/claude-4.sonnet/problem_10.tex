\section*{Problem 10}
If the sum of the first three terms of an increasing arithmetic sequence is 15 and their product is 105, find $n$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the three terms of the arithmetic sequence be $a-d$, $a$, and $a+d$, where $a$ is the middle term and $d > 0$ (since the sequence is increasing).

Given conditions:
1. Sum: $(a-d) + a + (a+d) = 15$
2. Product: $(a-d) \cdot a \cdot (a+d) = 105$

From condition 1:
\[
3a = 15 \Rightarrow a = 5
\]

From condition 2:
\[
(5-d) \cdot 5 \cdot (5+d) = 105
\]
\[
5(5-d)(5+d) = 105
\]
\[
5(25 - d^2) = 105
\]
\[
25 - d^2 = 21
\]
\[
d^2 = 4
\]
\[
d = 2 \text{ (since } d > 0 \text{)}
\]

So the three terms are:
- First term: $a - d = 5 - 2 = 3$
- Second term: $a = 5$
- Third term: $a + d = 5 + 2 = 7$

Let's verify:
- Sum: $3 + 5 + 7 = 15$ ✓
- Product: $3 \cdot 5 \cdot 7 = 105$ ✓
