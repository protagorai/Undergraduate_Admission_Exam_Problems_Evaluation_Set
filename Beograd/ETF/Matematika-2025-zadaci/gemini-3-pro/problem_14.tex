\section*{Problem 14}
Ugao između elipse $\frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{4} = 1$ i hiperbole $\frac{x^2}{4} - y^2 = 1$ u njihovoj presečnoj tački u prvom kvadrantu iznosi:

\subsection*{Solution}

Ellipse: $4x^2 + 9y^2 = 36$.
Hyperbola: $x^2 - 4y^2 = 4 \implies x^2 = 4+4y^2$.
Intersection:
$4(4+4y^2) + 9y^2 = 36 \implies 16 + 25y^2 = 36 \implies 25y^2 = 20 \implies y^2 = 4/5$.
$x^2 = 4 + 16/5 = 36/5$.
Derivatives (Slopes):
Ellipse: $\frac{2x}{9} + \frac{y}{2}y' = 0 \implies y'_E = -\frac{4x}{9y}$.
Hyperbola: $\frac{x}{2} - 2yy' = 0 \implies y'_H = \frac{x}{4y}$.
Product of slopes at intersection:
$y'_E \cdot y'_H = -\frac{4x}{9y} \cdot \frac{x}{4y} = -\frac{x^2}{9y^2}$.
Substitute $x^2/y^2 = (36/5)/(4/5) = 9$:
$y'_E \cdot y'_H = -\frac{9}{9} = -1$.
Since the product of slopes is -1, the curves are orthogonal. The angle is $90^\circ$.


\subsection*{Answer}
90^\circ (option \textbf{D}).
