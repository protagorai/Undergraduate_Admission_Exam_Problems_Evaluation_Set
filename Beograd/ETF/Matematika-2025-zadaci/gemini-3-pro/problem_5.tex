\section*{Problem 5}
Ako je $\sin \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}$, za $\alpha \in (\pi, 2\pi)$, onda je $\text{tg}\,\alpha$ jednako:

\subsection*{Solution}

We have $\sin \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}$.
Calculate $\cos \alpha$:
\[
\cos \alpha = 1 - 2\sin^2 \frac{\alpha}{2} = 1 - 2\left(\frac{1}{5}\right) = 1 - \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3}{5}.
\]
Since $\alpha \in (\pi, 2\pi)$ and $\cos \alpha > 0$, $\alpha$ must be in the 4th quadrant ($\frac{3\pi}{2} < \alpha < 2\pi$).
In the 4th quadrant, $\sin \alpha$ is negative.
\[
\sin \alpha = -\sqrt{1 - \cos^2 \alpha} = -\sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{3}{5}\right)^2} = -\sqrt{1 - \frac{9}{25}} = -\sqrt{\frac{16}{25}} = -\frac{4}{5}.
\]
Then $\text{tg}\,\alpha$ is:
\[
\text{tg}\,\alpha = \frac{\sin \alpha}{\cos \alpha} = \frac{-4/5}{3/5} = -\frac{4}{3}.
\]


\subsection*{Answer}
-\frac{4}{3} (option \textbf{B}).
