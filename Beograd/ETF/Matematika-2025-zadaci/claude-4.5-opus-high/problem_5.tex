\section*{Problem 5}
Ako je $\sin\frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}$, za $\alpha \in (\pi, 2\pi)$, onda je $\tan\alpha$ jednako:

\subsection*{Solution}
Dato je $\sin\frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}$ i $\alpha \in (\pi, 2\pi)$.

Pošto je $\alpha \in (\pi, 2\pi)$, sledi $\frac{\alpha}{2} \in \left(\frac{\pi}{2}, \pi\right)$, što znači da je $\frac{\alpha}{2}$ u drugom kvadrantu.

U drugom kvadrantu, sinus je pozitivan, a kosinus je negativan.

Koristimo identitet $\sin^2\frac{\alpha}{2} + \cos^2\frac{\alpha}{2} = 1$:
\[
\cos^2\frac{\alpha}{2} = 1 - \sin^2\frac{\alpha}{2} = 1 - \frac{1}{5} = \frac{4}{5}
\]

Pošto je $\frac{\alpha}{2}$ u drugom kvadrantu:
\[
\cos\frac{\alpha}{2} = -\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}
\]

Sada koristimo dvostruke uglove:
\[
\sin\alpha = 2\sin\frac{\alpha}{2}\cos\frac{\alpha}{2} = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \cdot \left(-\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\right) = -\frac{4}{5}
\]

\[
\cos\alpha = \cos^2\frac{\alpha}{2} - \sin^2\frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{4}{5} - \frac{1}{5} = \frac{3}{5}
\]

Dakle:
\[
\tan\alpha = \frac{\sin\alpha}{\cos\alpha} = \frac{-\frac{4}{5}}{\frac{3}{5}} = -\frac{4}{3}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-\frac{4}{3}$ (opcija \textbf{B}).
