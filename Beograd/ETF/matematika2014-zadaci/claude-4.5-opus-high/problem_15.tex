\section*{Problem 15}
Dat je polinom $P(x) = a_0x^4 + a_1x^3 + a_2x^2 + a_3x + a_4$ $(a_0, a_1, a_2, a_3, a_4 \in \mathbf{R}, a_0 \neq 0)$, takav da je $P(0) = P(1) = P(2) = P(-1) = 0$ i $P(-2) = 12$. Tada je $P(3)$ jednako:

(A) $\displaystyle\frac{1}{3}$ \quad (B) $\displaystyle-\frac{1}{2}$ \quad (C) $1$ \quad (D) $2$ \quad (E) $12$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
Since $P(0) = P(1) = P(2) = P(-1) = 0$, the polynomial has roots at $x = 0, 1, 2, -1$.

Since $P(x)$ is a degree 4 polynomial with leading coefficient $a_0$, we can write:
\[
P(x) = a_0 \cdot x(x-1)(x-2)(x+1)
\]

Using $P(-2) = 12$:
\[
P(-2) = a_0 \cdot (-2)(-2-1)(-2-2)(-2+1)
\]
\[
= a_0 \cdot (-2)(-3)(-4)(-1)
\]
\[
= a_0 \cdot (-2)(-3)(-4)(-1) = a_0 \cdot 24
\]

So $24a_0 = 12 \implies a_0 = \frac{1}{2}$.

Now compute $P(3)$:
\[
P(3) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3 \cdot (3-1) \cdot (3-2) \cdot (3+1)
\]
\[
= \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1 \cdot 4
\]
\[
= \frac{1}{2} \cdot 24 = 12
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$12$ (option \textbf{E}).
