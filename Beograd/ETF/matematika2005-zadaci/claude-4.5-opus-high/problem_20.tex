\section*{Problem 20}
Find the maximum area of a rectangle inscribed in the parabolic segment bounded by $y = 1 - x^2$ and the line $y = 0$, where one side of the rectangle lies on the $x$-axis.

\subsection*{Solution}
The parabola $y = 1 - x^2$ intersects the $x$-axis at $x = \pm 1$.

Let the rectangle have vertices at $(\pm a, 0)$ and $(\pm a, 1 - a^2)$ where $0 < a < 1$.

The width of the rectangle is $2a$ and the height is $1 - a^2$.

Area:
\[
A(a) = 2a(1 - a^2) = 2a - 2a^3
\]

To maximize, take the derivative and set it to zero:
\[
A'(a) = 2 - 6a^2 = 0
\]
\[
a^2 = \frac{1}{3}
\]
\[
a = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}
\]

The maximum area is:
\[
A = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \left(1 - \frac{1}{3}\right) = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} \cdot \frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{3\sqrt{3}} = \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{9}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\frac{4\sqrt{3}}{9}$ (option \textbf{B}).
