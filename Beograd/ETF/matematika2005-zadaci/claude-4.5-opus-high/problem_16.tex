\section*{Problem 16}
Find the coefficient of $a^8$ in the binomial expansion of $\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt[3]{a}} - a\right)^{12}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
We expand $\left(a^{-1/3} - a\right)^{12}$ using the binomial theorem:
\[
\left(a^{-1/3} - a\right)^{12} = \sum_{k=0}^{12} \binom{12}{k} (a^{-1/3})^{12-k} (-a)^k
\]
\[
= \sum_{k=0}^{12} \binom{12}{k} (-1)^k a^{-\frac{12-k}{3}} \cdot a^k
\]
\[
= \sum_{k=0}^{12} \binom{12}{k} (-1)^k a^{-\frac{12-k}{3} + k}
\]

The exponent of $a$ is:
\[
-\frac{12-k}{3} + k = -\frac{12-k}{3} + \frac{3k}{3} = \frac{-12+k+3k}{3} = \frac{4k-12}{3}
\]

We need this to equal 8:
\[
\frac{4k-12}{3} = 8 \implies 4k - 12 = 24 \implies 4k = 36 \implies k = 9
\]

The coefficient is:
\[
\binom{12}{9} (-1)^9 = -\binom{12}{9} = -\binom{12}{3} = -\frac{12 \cdot 11 \cdot 10}{3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1} = -\frac{1320}{6} = -220
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-220$ (option \textbf{C}).
