\section*{Problem 6}
If $\tan\alpha = -7$ and $\alpha \in (\pi/2, \pi)$, find $\displaystyle\frac{3\sin\alpha + \cos\alpha}{\cos\alpha - 3\sin\alpha}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Since $\tan\alpha = -7$, we have $\sin\alpha = -7\cos\alpha$ (but with signs appropriate for the second quadrant).

Divide numerator and denominator by $\cos\alpha$:
\[
\frac{3\sin\alpha + \cos\alpha}{\cos\alpha - 3\sin\alpha} = \frac{3\tan\alpha + 1}{1 - 3\tan\alpha}
\]

Substitute $\tan\alpha = -7$:
\[
= \frac{3(-7) + 1}{1 - 3(-7)} = \frac{-21 + 1}{1 + 21} = \frac{-20}{22} = -\frac{10}{11}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-\frac{10}{11}$ (option \textbf{B}).
