\section*{Problem 1}
Find the value of the expression:
\[
\frac{\sqrt{32}}{\sqrt{8}} + \sqrt{3} \cdot \sqrt{12} - \sqrt{50} \cdot \sqrt{2}
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Let's simplify each term:

\textbf{First term:}
\[
\frac{\sqrt{32}}{\sqrt{8}} = \sqrt{\frac{32}{8}} = \sqrt{4} = 2
\]

\textbf{Second term:}
\[
\sqrt{3} \cdot \sqrt{12} = \sqrt{3 \cdot 12} = \sqrt{36} = 6
\]

\textbf{Third term:}
\[
\sqrt{50} \cdot \sqrt{2} = \sqrt{50 \cdot 2} = \sqrt{100} = 10
\]

\textbf{Final calculation:}
\[
2 + 6 - 10 = -2
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-2$ (option \textbf{B}).
