\section*{Problem 4}
If a complex number $z$ satisfies $z + 2\bar{z} = 12 + 3i$ (where $i^2 = -1$), find $|z|$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $z = a + bi$ where $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$.

Then $\bar{z} = a - bi$.

Substituting into the equation:
\[
(a + bi) + 2(a - bi) = 12 + 3i
\]
\[
a + bi + 2a - 2bi = 12 + 3i
\]
\[
3a - bi = 12 + 3i
\]

Equating real and imaginary parts:
\begin{align*}
3a &= 12 \implies a = 4 \\
-b &= 3 \implies b = -3
\end{align*}

Therefore $z = 4 - 3i$.

The modulus is:
\[
|z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} = \sqrt{4^2 + (-3)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 9} = \sqrt{25} = 5
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$5$ (option \textbf{A}).
