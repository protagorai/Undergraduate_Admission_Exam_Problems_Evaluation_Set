\section*{Problem 9}
Find the sum of coefficients of tangent lines to the circle $x^2 + y^2 = 2$ that pass through the point of intersection with the line $x - y - 1 = 0$ and satisfy $x + y - 3 = 0$.

\subsection*{Solution}
The circle is $x^2 + y^2 = 2$ with center $(0,0)$ and radius $\sqrt{2}$.

The line $x - y - 1 = 0$ can be written as $y = x - 1$.

We need tangent lines to the circle that pass through a point on both lines.

First, find where the lines $x - y - 1 = 0$ and $x + y - 3 = 0$ intersect:
\begin{align*}
x - y &= 1 \\
x + y &= 3
\end{align*}

Adding: $2x = 4 \Rightarrow x = 2$, and $y = 1$.

So the point is $(2, 1)$.

Now find tangent lines from $(2,1)$ to circle $x^2 + y^2 = 2$.

Distance from $(2,1)$ to center $(0,0)$: $d = \sqrt{4+1} = \sqrt{5}$.

For a tangent line $y - 1 = m(x - 2)$, or $mx - y + (1 - 2m) = 0$.

Distance from $(0,0)$ to this line equals $\sqrt{2}$:
\[
\frac{|0 - 0 + (1-2m)|}{\sqrt{m^2 + 1}} = \sqrt{2}
\]
\[
\frac{|1-2m|}{\sqrt{m^2+1}} = \sqrt{2}
\]
\[
(1-2m)^2 = 2(m^2 + 1)
\]
\[
1 - 4m + 4m^2 = 2m^2 + 2
\]
\[
2m^2 - 4m - 1 = 0
\]

Sum of slopes (coefficients): $m_1 + m_2 = \frac{4}{2} = 2$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{A}).
