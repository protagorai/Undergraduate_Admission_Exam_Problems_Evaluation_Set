\subsection*{Solution}
Analyze the second equation:
\[ \log_4 y \cdot \log_y (y-3x+6) = 1 \]
Using the base change formula $\log_y A = \frac{\log_4 A}{\log_4 y}$, we get:
\[ \log_4 y \cdot \frac{\log_4 (y-3x+6)}{\log_4 y} = 1 \]
\[ \log_4 (y-3x+6) = 1 \implies y-3x+6 = 4 \implies y = 3x-2. \]
Constraints:
1. $y > 0, y \neq 1$.
2. $y-3x+6 > 0 \implies 4 > 0$ (satisfied).
From $y > 0 \implies 3x-2 > 0 \implies x > 2/3$.
From $y \neq 1 \implies 3x-2 \neq 1 \implies x \neq 1$.

Now substitute into the first equation. Let $u = |x|-2$.
Since $x > 2/3$, $|x| = x$. So $u = x-2$.
For the log base and power base to be valid, we need $u > 0 \implies x > 2$.
Equation 1 becomes:
\[ y \cdot u^{\log_y u} = u^{5/2}. \]
Take $\log_y$ of both sides:
\[ \log_y y + \log_y (u^{\log_y u}) = \log_y (u^{5/2}) \]
\[ 1 + (\log_y u)(\log_y u) = \frac{5}{2} \log_y u. \]
Let $z = \log_y u$.
\[ z^2 - \frac{5}{2}z + 1 = 0 \implies 2z^2 - 5z + 2 = 0 \implies (2z-1)(z-2)=0. \]
Solutions are $z = 1/2$ and $z = 2$.

Case 1: $\log_y u = 1/2 \implies u = y^{1/2} = \sqrt{y}$.
Substitute $u=x-2$ and $y=3x-2$:
\[ x-2 = \sqrt{3x-2}. \]
Square both sides:
\[ (x-2)^2 = 3x-2 \implies x^2 - 4x + 4 = 3x - 2 \implies x^2 - 7x + 6 = 0. \]
$(x-1)(x-6) = 0$.
$x=1$ or $x=6$.
Since $x > 2$, $x=1$ is rejected.
$x=6 \implies y = 3(6)-2 = 16$.
Check condition $x>2$: $6>2$. Valid.

Case 2: $\log_y u = 2 \implies u = y^2$.
Substitute:
\[ x-2 = (3x-2)^2 = 9x^2 - 12x + 4. \]
\[ 9x^2 - 13x + 6 = 0. \]
Discriminant $D = (-13)^2 - 4(9)(6) = 169 - 216 = -47 < 0$.
No real solutions.

Thus, there is only 1 solution $(x, y) = (6, 16)$.

\subsection*{Answer}
1 (option \textbf{C}).
