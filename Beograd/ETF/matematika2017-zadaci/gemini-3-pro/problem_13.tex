\section*{Problem 13}
Oštar ugao pod kojim se seku tangenta krive $2x^2 + 3y^2 = 5$ u njenoj tački $(1, 1)$ i tangenta krive $x^2 - 4x + y^2 + 6y = 3$ u njenoj tački $(2, 1)$ jednak je:
(A) $\text{arctg}\frac{1}{2}$
(B) $\text{arctg}\frac{3}{2}$
(C) $\text{arctg}\frac{2}{3}$
(D) $\text{arctg}\frac{1}{6}$
(E) $\text{arctg}\frac{3}{5}$
(N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
Find the slopes of the tangents at the given points.

\textbf{Curve 1}: $2x^2 + 3y^2 = 5$ at $M_1(1, 1)$.
Differentiate implicitly with respect to $x$:
\[ 4x + 6y y' = 0 \implies y' = -\frac{4x}{6y} = -\frac{2x}{3y}. \]
At $(1, 1)$, the slope is $k_1 = -\frac{2(1)}{3(1)} = -\frac{2}{3}$.

\textbf{Curve 2}: $x^2 - 4x + y^2 + 6y = 3$ at $M_2(2, 1)$.
Differentiate implicitly:
\[ 2x - 4 + 2y y' + 6y' = 0 \implies 2(x-2) + y'(2y+6) = 0 \implies y' = -\frac{2(x-2)}{2(y+3)} = -\frac{x-2}{y+3}. \]
At $(2, 1)$, the slope is $k_2 = -\frac{2-2}{1+3} = 0$.

\textbf{Angle}:
The angle $\varphi$ between two lines with slopes $k_1$ and $k_2$ is given by:
\[ \tan \varphi = \left| \frac{k_2 - k_1}{1 + k_1 k_2} \right|. \]
Substitute $k_1 = -2/3$ and $k_2 = 0$:
\[ \tan \varphi = \left| \frac{0 - (-2/3)}{1 + (-2/3)(0)} \right| = \left| \frac{2/3}{1} \right| = \frac{2}{3}. \]
Thus, $\varphi = \text{arctg}\frac{2}{3}$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$\text{arctg}\frac{2}{3}$ (option \textbf{C}).
