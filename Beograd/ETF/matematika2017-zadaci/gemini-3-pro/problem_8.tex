\section*{Problem 8}
Ako se razvije izraz $2x (1 + \sqrt[3]{x})^{10} + x^4 (x^2 + \frac{1}{\sqrt[5]{x^2}})^{12} + 5x^2 (\sqrt[6]{x} - \frac{1}{\sqrt[6]{x}})^{18}$, ($x \in \mathbf{R}^+$) i jedan od sabiraka je $A \cdot x^4$, tada je $A$ jednako:
(A) $-3994$
(B) 2122
(C) $-2451$
(D) 1326
(E) $-1452$
(N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
We look for the coefficient of $x^4$ in each term of the sum.
The expression is $S = S_1 + S_2 + S_3$.

\textbf{1. Term $S_1 = 2x (1 + x^{1/3})^{10}$}:
The general term of $(1 + x^{1/3})^{10}$ is $\binom{10}{k} (x^{1/3})^k = \binom{10}{k} x^{k/3}$.
Multiplying by $2x$, the term becomes $2 \binom{10}{k} x^{1 + k/3}$.
For the power to be $x^4$, we need $1 + k/3 = 4 \implies k/3 = 3 \implies k = 9$.
The coefficient is $A_1 = 2 \binom{10}{9} = 2 \cdot 10 = 20$.

\textbf{2. Term $S_2 = x^4 (x^2 + x^{-2/5})^{12}$}:
The general term of the binomial expansion is $\binom{12}{k} (x^2)^{12-k} (x^{-2/5})^k = \binom{12}{k} x^{24-2k} x^{-0.4k} = \binom{12}{k} x^{24-2.4k}$.
Multiplying by $x^4$, the term becomes $\binom{12}{k} x^{28-2.4k}$.
For the power to be $x^4$, we need $28 - 2.4k = 4 \implies 2.4k = 24 \implies k = 10$.
The coefficient is $A_2 = \binom{12}{10} = \binom{12}{2} = \frac{12 \cdot 11}{2} = 66$.

\textbf{3. Term $S_3 = 5x^2 (x^{1/6} - x^{-1/6})^{18}$}:
The general term of the binomial is $\binom{18}{k} (x^{1/6})^{18-k} (-x^{-1/6})^k = \binom{18}{k} (-1)^k x^{3 - k/6 - k/6} = \binom{18}{k} (-1)^k x^{3 - k/3}$.
Multiplying by $5x^2$, the term becomes $5 \binom{18}{k} (-1)^k x^{5 - k/3}$.
For the power to be $x^4$, we need $5 - k/3 = 4 \implies k/3 = 1 \implies k = 3$.
The coefficient is $A_3 = 5 \binom{18}{3} (-1)^3 = -5 \cdot \frac{18 \cdot 17 \cdot 16}{3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1} = -5 \cdot (3 \cdot 17 \cdot 16) = -5 \cdot 816 = -4080$.

\textbf{Total Coefficient $A$}:
$A = A_1 + A_2 + A_3 = 20 + 66 - 4080 = 86 - 4080 = -3994$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$-3994$ (option \textbf{A}).
