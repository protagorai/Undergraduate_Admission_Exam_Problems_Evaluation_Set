\section*{Problem 18}
Na koliko naƒçina se mogu izabrati tri broja iz skupa prirodnih brojeva $\{1, 2, 3, \dots, 40\}$ tako da im zbir bude neparan broj?
\[ (A)\ 1140 \quad (B)\ 3800 \quad (C)\ 6480 \quad (D)\ 4940 \quad (E)\ 14080 \quad (N)\ \text{Ne znam} \]

\subsection*{Solution}
The set contains 20 odd numbers ($1, 3, \dots, 39$) and 20 even numbers ($2, 4, \dots, 40$).
The sum of three numbers is odd if either:
1. All three are odd (Odd + Odd + Odd = Odd).
2. Two are even and one is odd (Even + Even + Odd = Odd).

Case 1: 3 odd numbers.
Choose 3 from 20 odds: $\binom{20}{3} = \frac{20 \cdot 19 \cdot 18}{6} = 20 \cdot 19 \cdot 3 = 1140$.

Case 2: 2 even and 1 odd.
Choose 2 from 20 evens and 1 from 20 odds: $\binom{20}{2} \binom{20}{1} = \frac{20 \cdot 19}{2} \cdot 20 = 190 \cdot 20 = 3800$.

Total ways = $1140 + 3800 = 4940$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$4940$ (option \textbf{D}).
