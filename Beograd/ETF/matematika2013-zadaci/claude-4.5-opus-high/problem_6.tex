\section*{Problem 6}
If $\displaystyle\frac{\cos(\alpha + \beta)}{\cos(\alpha - \beta)} = \frac{1}{3}$ (where $\alpha, \beta \neq \frac{\pi}{2} + k\pi$, $\alpha - \beta \neq \frac{\pi}{2} + m\pi$, $k, m \in \mathbf{Z}$), find $\tan\alpha \cdot \tan\beta$.

Options: (A) $\frac{1}{3}$ \quad (B) $\frac{1}{2}$ \quad (C) $1$ \quad (D) $\frac{1}{4}$ \quad (E) $\frac{2}{3}$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
Using the cosine addition formulas:
\begin{align*}
\cos(\alpha + \beta) &= \cos\alpha\cos\beta - \sin\alpha\sin\beta \\
\cos(\alpha - \beta) &= \cos\alpha\cos\beta + \sin\alpha\sin\beta
\end{align*}

Given:
\[
\frac{\cos\alpha\cos\beta - \sin\alpha\sin\beta}{\cos\alpha\cos\beta + \sin\alpha\sin\beta} = \frac{1}{3}
\]

Cross-multiplying:
\[
3(\cos\alpha\cos\beta - \sin\alpha\sin\beta) = \cos\alpha\cos\beta + \sin\alpha\sin\beta
\]
\[
3\cos\alpha\cos\beta - 3\sin\alpha\sin\beta = \cos\alpha\cos\beta + \sin\alpha\sin\beta
\]
\[
2\cos\alpha\cos\beta = 4\sin\alpha\sin\beta
\]
\[
\frac{\sin\alpha\sin\beta}{\cos\alpha\cos\beta} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}
\]

Therefore:
\[
\tan\alpha \cdot \tan\beta = \frac{1}{2}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\frac{1}{2}$ (option \textbf{B}).
