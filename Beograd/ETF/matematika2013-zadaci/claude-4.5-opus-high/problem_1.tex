\section*{Problem 1}
Find the value of the expression $2x^2 - 2{,}4x - 1{,}7$ for $x = 7 \cdot 10^{-1}$.

Options: (A) 1 \quad (B) $-17{,}52$ \quad (C) $6{,}42$ \quad (D) $-2{,}40$ \quad (E) $-2{,}89$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
First, we note that $x = 7 \cdot 10^{-1} = 0{,}7$.

Now we substitute into the expression:
\[
2x^2 - 2{,}4x - 1{,}7 = 2(0{,}7)^2 - 2{,}4 \cdot 0{,}7 - 1{,}7
\]

Calculate step by step:
\begin{align*}
(0{,}7)^2 &= 0{,}49 \\
2 \cdot 0{,}49 &= 0{,}98 \\
2{,}4 \cdot 0{,}7 &= 1{,}68
\end{align*}

Therefore:
\[
2x^2 - 2{,}4x - 1{,}7 = 0{,}98 - 1{,}68 - 1{,}7 = 0{,}98 - 3{,}38 = -2{,}40
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-2{,}40$ (option \textbf{D}).
