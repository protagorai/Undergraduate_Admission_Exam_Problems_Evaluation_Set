\section*{Problem 9}
If the polynomial $P(x) = x^{2014} + px^{2013} + qx - 1$ (where $p, q \in \mathbf{R}$) is divisible by $(x + 1)$, find the sum $p + q$.

Options: (A) $0$ \quad (B) $1$ \quad (C) $2$ \quad (D) $-2$ \quad (E) $-1$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
If $P(x)$ is divisible by $(x + 1)$, then by the Factor Theorem, $P(-1) = 0$.

Calculate $P(-1)$:
\[
P(-1) = (-1)^{2014} + p(-1)^{2013} + q(-1) - 1
\]

Since $2014$ is even: $(-1)^{2014} = 1$

Since $2013$ is odd: $(-1)^{2013} = -1$

Therefore:
\[
P(-1) = 1 + p(-1) + q(-1) - 1 = 1 - p - q - 1 = -p - q
\]

Setting $P(-1) = 0$:
\[
-p - q = 0
\]
\[
p + q = 0
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$0$ (option \textbf{A}).
