\section*{Problem 18}
In how many ways can three numbers be chosen from the set of natural numbers $\{1, 2, 3, \ldots, 40\}$ such that their sum is odd?

Options: (A) $1140$ \quad (B) $3800$ \quad (C) $6480$ \quad (D) $4940$ \quad (E) $14080$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
The set $\{1, 2, 3, \ldots, 40\}$ contains:
\begin{itemize}
\item 20 odd numbers: $\{1, 3, 5, \ldots, 39\}$
\item 20 even numbers: $\{2, 4, 6, \ldots, 40\}$
\end{itemize}

For the sum of three numbers to be odd, we need either:
\begin{enumerate}
\item All three numbers are odd (odd + odd + odd = odd)
\item Exactly one number is odd and two are even (odd + even + even = odd)
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Case 1: Three odd numbers}

Number of ways $= \binom{20}{3} = \frac{20 \cdot 19 \cdot 18}{6} = \frac{6840}{6} = 1140$

\textbf{Case 2: One odd and two even numbers}

Number of ways $= \binom{20}{1} \cdot \binom{20}{2} = 20 \cdot \frac{20 \cdot 19}{2} = 20 \cdot 190 = 3800$

\textbf{Total:}
\[
1140 + 3800 = 4940
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$4940$ (option \textbf{D}).
