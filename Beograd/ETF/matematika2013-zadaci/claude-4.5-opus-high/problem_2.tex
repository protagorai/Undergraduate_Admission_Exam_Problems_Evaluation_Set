\section*{Problem 2}
Find the equation of the line passing through points $M_1(-1, 1)$ and $M_2(2, 4)$.

Options: (A) $x - y + 2 = 0$ \quad (B) $x + y = 0$ \quad (C) $-2x + y = 0$ \quad (D) $-3x - y - 2 = 0$ \quad (E) $x - y - 2 = 0$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
The equation of a line through two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ can be found using:
\[
\frac{x - x_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{y - y_1}{y_2 - y_1}
\]

With $M_1(-1, 1)$ and $M_2(2, 4)$:
\[
\frac{x - (-1)}{2 - (-1)} = \frac{y - 1}{4 - 1}
\]
\[
\frac{x + 1}{3} = \frac{y - 1}{3}
\]

Cross-multiplying:
\[
x + 1 = y - 1
\]
\[
x - y + 2 = 0
\]

Let's verify: For $M_1(-1, 1)$: $-1 - 1 + 2 = 0$ \checkmark

For $M_2(2, 4)$: $2 - 4 + 2 = 0$ \checkmark

\subsection*{Answer}
$x - y + 2 = 0$ (option \textbf{A}).
