\section*{Problem 11}
Let $x_1$ and $x_2$ be roots of the equation $x^2 + bx + c = 0$ (where $b, c \in \mathbf{R} \setminus \{0\}$). Find the expression $x_1^4 + x_2^4$.

Options: (A) $b^4 - 4b^2c + 2c^2$ \quad (B) $b^4 - 4b^2c^2 + 2c^2$ \quad (C) $b^4 - 4bc^2 + 2c^2$ \quad (D) $b^4 + 4c^2$ \quad (E) $b^4 - 4b^2c + 2c$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
By Vieta's formulas:
\begin{align*}
x_1 + x_2 &= -b \\
x_1 x_2 &= c
\end{align*}

First, find $x_1^2 + x_2^2$:
\[
x_1^2 + x_2^2 = (x_1 + x_2)^2 - 2x_1x_2 = b^2 - 2c
\]

Next, find $x_1^4 + x_2^4$:
\[
x_1^4 + x_2^4 = (x_1^2 + x_2^2)^2 - 2(x_1x_2)^2 = (b^2 - 2c)^2 - 2c^2
\]

Expanding:
\[
= b^4 - 4b^2c + 4c^2 - 2c^2 = b^4 - 4b^2c + 2c^2
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$b^4 - 4b^2c + 2c^2$ (option \textbf{A}).
