\section*{Problem 15}
Find the interval containing all real solutions of the equation $4^x - 7 \cdot 2^{\frac{x-3}{2}} = 2^{-x}$.

Options: (A) $(-9, -2]$ \quad (B) $(0, 3]$ \quad (C) $(-2, 0]$ \quad (D) $(7, 12]$ \quad (E) $(3, 7]$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
Rewrite the equation using $4^x = 2^{2x}$ and $2^{\frac{x-3}{2}} = 2^{x/2} \cdot 2^{-3/2} = \frac{2^{x/2}}{2\sqrt{2}}$:

\[
2^{2x} - 7 \cdot 2^{\frac{x-3}{2}} = 2^{-x}
\]

Let $t = 2^{x/2}$, so $t > 0$, $2^x = t^2$, $2^{2x} = t^4$, and $2^{-x} = t^{-2}$.

Also, $2^{\frac{x-3}{2}} = 2^{x/2} \cdot 2^{-3/2} = \frac{t}{2^{3/2}} = \frac{t}{2\sqrt{2}}$.

The equation becomes:
\[
t^4 - 7 \cdot \frac{t}{2\sqrt{2}} = \frac{1}{t^2}
\]

Multiply through by $t^2$:
\[
t^6 - \frac{7t^3}{2\sqrt{2}} = 1
\]

Let $u = t^3$:
\[
u^2 - \frac{7u}{2\sqrt{2}} - 1 = 0
\]

Multiply by $2\sqrt{2}$:
\[
2\sqrt{2}u^2 - 7u - 2\sqrt{2} = 0
\]

Using the quadratic formula:
\[
u = \frac{7 \pm \sqrt{49 + 16 \cdot 2}}{4\sqrt{2}} = \frac{7 \pm \sqrt{49 + 32}}{4\sqrt{2}} = \frac{7 \pm 9}{4\sqrt{2}}
\]

So $u = \frac{16}{4\sqrt{2}} = \frac{4}{\sqrt{2}} = 2\sqrt{2}$ or $u = \frac{-2}{4\sqrt{2}} = \frac{-1}{2\sqrt{2}} < 0$.

Since $u = t^3 > 0$, we have $u = 2\sqrt{2} = 2^{3/2}$.

Therefore $t^3 = 2^{3/2}$, so $t = 2^{1/2} = \sqrt{2}$.

Since $t = 2^{x/2}$:
\[
2^{x/2} = 2^{1/2} \Rightarrow x/2 = 1/2 \Rightarrow x = 1
\]

Check: $x = 1$ is in interval $(0, 3]$.

Verify: $4^1 - 7 \cdot 2^{(1-3)/2} = 4 - 7 \cdot 2^{-1} = 4 - 3.5 = 0.5$ and $2^{-1} = 0.5$. \checkmark

\subsection*{Answer}
$(0, 3]$ (option \textbf{B}).
