\section*{Problem 10}
Prave $-ax + y - 3 = 0$, $x - by + 2 = 0$ seku se u centru kruga $x^2 + y^2 - 2x + 4y - 10 = 0$. Ugao između ovih pravih je:

(A) $60°$ \quad (B) $30°$ \quad (C) $90°$ \quad (D) $45°$ \quad (E) $75°$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
First, let's find the center of the circle by rewriting the equation in standard form.

Starting with $x^2 + y^2 - 2x + 4y - 10 = 0$, we complete the square:
\begin{align}
x^2 - 2x + y^2 + 4y &= 10\\
(x^2 - 2x + 1) + (y^2 + 4y + 4) &= 10 + 1 + 4\\
(x - 1)^2 + (y + 2)^2 &= 15
\end{align}

So the center of the circle is $(1, -2)$.

Since the lines intersect at the center, we have:
\begin{align}
-a(1) + (-2) - 3 &= 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad -a - 5 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad a = -5\\
(1) - b(-2) + 2 &= 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 1 + 2b + 2 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad b = -\frac{3}{2}
\end{align}

So our lines are:
\begin{align}
L_1: 5x + y - 3 &= 0\\
L_2: x + \frac{3}{2}y + 2 &= 0 \quad \text{or} \quad 2x + 3y + 4 = 0
\end{align}

To find the angle between two lines $A_1x + B_1y + C_1 = 0$ and $A_2x + B_2y + C_2 = 0$, we use:
$$\cos \theta = \frac{|A_1A_2 + B_1B_2|}{\sqrt{A_1^2 + B_1^2}\sqrt{A_2^2 + B_2^2}}$$

For our lines:
- $L_1: 5x + y - 3 = 0$ has $(A_1, B_1) = (5, 1)$
- $L_2: 2x + 3y + 4 = 0$ has $(A_2, B_2) = (2, 3)$

\begin{align}
\cos \theta &= \frac{|5 \cdot 2 + 1 \cdot 3|}{\sqrt{5^2 + 1^2}\sqrt{2^2 + 3^2}}\\
&= \frac{|10 + 3|}{\sqrt{26}\sqrt{13}}\\
&= \frac{13}{\sqrt{26 \cdot 13}}\\
&= \frac{13}{\sqrt{338}}\\
&= \frac{13}{13\sqrt{2}}\\
&= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\\
&= \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}
\end{align}

Since $\cos \theta = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$, we have $\theta = 45°$.

\subsection*{Answer}
(D) $45°$
