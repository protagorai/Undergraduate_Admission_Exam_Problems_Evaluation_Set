\section*{Problem 17}
Maksimalni obim pravougaonika upisanog u krug datog poluprečnika $r$ iznosi:

(A) $5\sqrt{2}r$ \quad (B) $4\sqrt{2}r$ \quad (C) $\sqrt{2}r$ \quad (D) $3\sqrt{2}r$ \quad (E) Nijedan od ponuđenih odgovora \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
Let's place the circle at the origin with radius $r$. A rectangle inscribed in this circle will have its vertices on the circle.

Due to symmetry, we can assume the rectangle has sides parallel to the coordinate axes. If the rectangle has vertices at $(\pm a, \pm b)$, then since these points lie on the circle:
$$a^2 + b^2 = r^2$$

The perimeter of the rectangle is:
$$P = 2(2a + 2b) = 4(a + b)$$

We want to maximize $P = 4(a + b)$ subject to the constraint $a^2 + b^2 = r^2$.

Using the method of Lagrange multipliers, or alternatively, we can use the constraint to express one variable in terms of the other:
$$b = \sqrt{r^2 - a^2}$$

So we want to maximize:
$$f(a) = 4(a + \sqrt{r^2 - a^2})$$

Taking the derivative:
$$f'(a) = 4\left(1 + \frac{-a}{\sqrt{r^2 - a^2}}\right) = 4\left(1 - \frac{a}{\sqrt{r^2 - a^2}}\right)$$

Setting $f'(a) = 0$:
$$1 - \frac{a}{\sqrt{r^2 - a^2}} = 0$$
$$\frac{a}{\sqrt{r^2 - a^2}} = 1$$
$$a = \sqrt{r^2 - a^2}$$
$$a^2 = r^2 - a^2$$
$$2a^2 = r^2$$
$$a = \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}}$$

Similarly, $b = \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}}$.

So the maximum perimeter occurs when the rectangle is actually a square with side length $\frac{2r}{\sqrt{2}} = r\sqrt{2}$.

The maximum perimeter is:
$$P = 4 \cdot r\sqrt{2} = 4r\sqrt{2}$$

\subsection*{Answer}
(B) $4\sqrt{2}r$
