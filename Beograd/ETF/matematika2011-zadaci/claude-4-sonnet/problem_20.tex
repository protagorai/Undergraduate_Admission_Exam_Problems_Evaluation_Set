\section*{Problem 20}
Skup svih realnih vrednosti $x$ za koje važi nejednakost $\frac{3 \cdot 3^{2x} - 4 \cdot 4^{2x}}{-1 + 5^{x+1}} < 4$ je oblika (za neke realne brojeve $a, b$ takve da je $-\infty < a < b < +\infty$):

(A) $(0, a)$ \quad (B) $(-\infty, a) \cup (b, +\infty)$ \quad (C) $[a, b]$ \quad (D) $(a, b)$ \quad (E) $(a, b) \cup (b, +\infty)$ \quad (N) Ne znam

\subsection*{Solution}
We need to solve:
$$\frac{3 \cdot 3^{2x} - 4 \cdot 4^{2x}}{-1 + 5^{x+1}} < 4$$

First, let's simplify the expression:
- $3 \cdot 3^{2x} = 3^{1+2x} = 3^{2x+1}$
- $4 \cdot 4^{2x} = 4^{1+2x} = 4^{2x+1}$
- $5^{x+1} = 5 \cdot 5^x$

So our inequality becomes:
$$\frac{3^{2x+1} - 4^{2x+1}}{5 \cdot 5^x - 1} < 4$$

Let's substitute $y = 5^x$ (so $y > 0$). Then:
- $3^{2x+1} = 3 \cdot (3^2)^x = 3 \cdot 9^x$
- $4^{2x+1} = 4 \cdot (4^2)^x = 4 \cdot 16^x$

We need to express $9^x$ and $16^x$ in terms of $y = 5^x$.

Since $x = \log_5 y$:
- $9^x = 9^{\log_5 y} = y^{\log_5 9}$
- $16^x = 16^{\log_5 y} = y^{\log_5 16}$

This becomes quite complex. Let's try a different approach.

Let's substitute $t = 5^x$ where $t > 0$. Then $x = \log_5 t$.

The inequality becomes:
$$\frac{3 \cdot 9^{\log_5 t} - 4 \cdot 16^{\log_5 t}}{5t - 1} < 4$$

Using the property $a^{\log_b c} = c^{\log_b a}$:
- $9^{\log_5 t} = t^{\log_5 9}$
- $16^{\log_5 t} = t^{\log_5 16}$

Let $\alpha = \log_5 9$ and $\beta = \log_5 16$. Then:
$$\frac{3t^\alpha - 4t^\beta}{5t - 1} < 4$$

This is still complex. Let's try specific values to understand the behavior.

When $x = 0$: $t = 5^0 = 1$
$$\frac{3 \cdot 1 - 4 \cdot 1}{5 \cdot 1 - 1} = \frac{-1}{4} = -\frac{1}{4} < 4$$ ✓

When $x = 1$: $t = 5^1 = 5$
$$\frac{3 \cdot 9 - 4 \cdot 16}{5 \cdot 5 - 1} = \frac{27 - 64}{24} = \frac{-37}{24} < 4$$ ✓

The denominator $5t - 1 = 0$ when $t = \frac{1}{5}$, i.e., when $x = \log_5(\frac{1}{5}) = -1$.

As $x \to +\infty$, the terms $3^{2x+1}$ and $4^{2x+1}$ grow much faster than $5^{x+1}$, and since $4^{2x+1}$ grows faster than $3^{2x+1}$, the numerator becomes very negative while the denominator becomes very positive, so the fraction approaches $-\infty < 4$.

As $x \to -\infty$, all exponential terms approach 0, so the fraction approaches $\frac{0}{-1} = 0 < 4$.

The critical point is at $x = -1$ where the denominator is zero.

Based on the analysis and the behavior of the function, the solution appears to be an interval of the form $(a, b)$.

\subsection*{Answer}
(D) $(a, b)$
