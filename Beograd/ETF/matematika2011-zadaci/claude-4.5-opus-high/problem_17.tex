\section*{Problem 17}
Find the maximum perimeter of a rectangle inscribed in a circle with radius $r$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the rectangle have sides $a$ and $b$. Since it's inscribed in a circle of radius $r$, the diagonal of the rectangle equals the diameter $2r$.

By the Pythagorean theorem:
\[
a^2 + b^2 = (2r)^2 = 4r^2
\]

The perimeter is $P = 2(a + b)$. We want to maximize $a + b$ subject to $a^2 + b^2 = 4r^2$.

Using the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality:
\[
(a + b)^2 \leq 2(a^2 + b^2) = 2 \cdot 4r^2 = 8r^2
\]

Therefore $a + b \leq 2\sqrt{2}r$, with equality when $a = b$.

When $a = b$: $2a^2 = 4r^2 \Rightarrow a = \sqrt{2}r$.

The maximum perimeter is:
\[
P_{\max} = 2(a + b) = 2 \cdot 2\sqrt{2}r = 4\sqrt{2}r
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
\textbf{(B)} $4\sqrt{2}r$
