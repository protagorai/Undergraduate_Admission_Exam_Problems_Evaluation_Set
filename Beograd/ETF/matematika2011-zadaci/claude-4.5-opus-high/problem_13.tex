\section*{Problem 13}
Find the remainder when the polynomial $x^{243} + x^{81} + x^{27} + x^9 + x^3 + x$ is divided by $x^2 - 1$.

\subsection*{Solution}
When dividing a polynomial $P(x)$ by $x^2 - 1$, the remainder is a polynomial of degree at most 1, say $R(x) = ax + b$.

Since $x^2 - 1 = (x-1)(x+1)$, we have:
\[
P(x) = (x^2 - 1) \cdot Q(x) + ax + b
\]

This means $P(1) = a + b$ and $P(-1) = -a + b$.

\textbf{Calculate $P(1)$:}
\[
P(1) = 1^{243} + 1^{81} + 1^{27} + 1^9 + 1^3 + 1 = 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = 6
\]

\textbf{Calculate $P(-1)$:}
\[
P(-1) = (-1)^{243} + (-1)^{81} + (-1)^{27} + (-1)^9 + (-1)^3 + (-1)
\]
All exponents are odd, so each term equals $-1$:
\[
P(-1) = -1 + (-1) + (-1) + (-1) + (-1) + (-1) = -6
\]

Now solve for $a$ and $b$:
\begin{align*}
a + b &= 6 \\
-a + b &= -6
\end{align*}

Adding: $2b = 0 \Rightarrow b = 0$

Subtracting: $2a = 12 \Rightarrow a = 6$

Therefore, the remainder is $R(x) = 6x$.

\subsection*{Answer}
\textbf{(E)} $6x$
