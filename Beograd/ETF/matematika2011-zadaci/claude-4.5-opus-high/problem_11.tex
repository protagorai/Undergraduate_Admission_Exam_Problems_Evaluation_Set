\section*{Problem 11}
Find the total number of real solutions of the system:
\[
\frac{x+y}{x-y} + \frac{x-y}{x+y} = \frac{5}{2}, \quad x^2 + y^2 = 20
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $u = \frac{x+y}{x-y}$. Then the first equation becomes:
\[
u + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{5}{2}
\]
\[
\frac{u^2 + 1}{u} = \frac{5}{2}
\]
\[
2u^2 + 2 = 5u
\]
\[
2u^2 - 5u + 2 = 0
\]
\[
(2u - 1)(u - 2) = 0
\]

So $u = \frac{1}{2}$ or $u = 2$.

\textbf{Case 1:} $\frac{x+y}{x-y} = 2$

$x + y = 2(x - y) = 2x - 2y$

$3y = x$, so $x = 3y$

Substituting into $x^2 + y^2 = 20$:
\[
9y^2 + y^2 = 20 \Rightarrow 10y^2 = 20 \Rightarrow y^2 = 2 \Rightarrow y = \pm\sqrt{2}
\]

This gives solutions $(3\sqrt{2}, \sqrt{2})$ and $(-3\sqrt{2}, -\sqrt{2})$.

\textbf{Case 2:} $\frac{x+y}{x-y} = \frac{1}{2}$

$2(x + y) = x - y$

$2x + 2y = x - y$

$x = -3y$

Substituting into $x^2 + y^2 = 20$:
\[
9y^2 + y^2 = 20 \Rightarrow 10y^2 = 20 \Rightarrow y^2 = 2 \Rightarrow y = \pm\sqrt{2}
\]

This gives solutions $(-3\sqrt{2}, \sqrt{2})$ and $(3\sqrt{2}, -\sqrt{2})$.

We need to verify these don't make denominators zero ($x \neq y$ and $x \neq -y$):
- For $x = 3y$: $x - y = 2y \neq 0$ (when $y \neq 0$), $x + y = 4y \neq 0$ ✓
- For $x = -3y$: $x - y = -4y \neq 0$, $x + y = -2y \neq 0$ ✓

Total: \textbf{4} real solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
\textbf{(C)} 4
