\section*{Problem 7}
If $x_1$ and $x_2$ are roots of the quadratic equation $\displaystyle\frac{1}{x-1} + \frac{1}{x-2} = 1$, then find the value of $\displaystyle\frac{x_1}{x_2} + \frac{x_2}{x_1}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
First, let's solve for the quadratic equation. Multiply both sides by $(x-1)(x-2)$:
\[
(x-2) + (x-1) = (x-1)(x-2)
\]
\[
2x - 3 = x^2 - 3x + 2
\]
\[
x^2 - 5x + 5 = 0
\]

By Vieta's formulas:
\begin{align*}
x_1 + x_2 &= 5 \\
x_1 \cdot x_2 &= 5
\end{align*}

Now we calculate:
\[
\frac{x_1}{x_2} + \frac{x_2}{x_1} = \frac{x_1^2 + x_2^2}{x_1 x_2}
\]

We need $x_1^2 + x_2^2$:
\[
x_1^2 + x_2^2 = (x_1 + x_2)^2 - 2x_1 x_2 = 5^2 - 2(5) = 25 - 10 = 15
\]

Therefore:
\[
\frac{x_1}{x_2} + \frac{x_2}{x_1} = \frac{15}{5} = 3
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
\textbf{(D)} 3
