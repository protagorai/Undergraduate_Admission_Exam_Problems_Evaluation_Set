\section*{Problem 5}
Find the sum $\displaystyle\sin\frac{3\pi}{7} + \sin\frac{4\pi}{7}$.

\subsection*{Solution}
We use the sum-to-product formula:
\[
\sin A + \sin B = 2\sin\frac{A+B}{2}\cos\frac{A-B}{2}
\]

With $A = \frac{3\pi}{7}$ and $B = \frac{4\pi}{7}$:
\begin{align*}
\frac{A + B}{2} &= \frac{\frac{3\pi}{7} + \frac{4\pi}{7}}{2} = \frac{\frac{7\pi}{7}}{2} = \frac{\pi}{2} \\
\frac{A - B}{2} &= \frac{\frac{3\pi}{7} - \frac{4\pi}{7}}{2} = \frac{-\frac{\pi}{7}}{2} = -\frac{\pi}{14}
\end{align*}

Therefore:
\[
\sin\frac{3\pi}{7} + \sin\frac{4\pi}{7} = 2\sin\frac{\pi}{2}\cos\left(-\frac{\pi}{14}\right) = 2 \cdot 1 \cdot \cos\frac{\pi}{14} = 2\cos\frac{\pi}{14}
\]

(Note: $\cos(-\theta) = \cos\theta$)

\subsection*{Answer}
\textbf{(D)} $2\cos\frac{\pi}{14}$
