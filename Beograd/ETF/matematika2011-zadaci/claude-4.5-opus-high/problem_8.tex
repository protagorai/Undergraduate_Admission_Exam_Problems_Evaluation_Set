\section*{Problem 8}
Let $\alpha$, $\beta$, and $\gamma$ be angles in an arbitrary triangle, and let $a$, $b$, $c$ be the lengths of the sides opposite to these angles, with $R$ being the circumradius. Find the value of:
\[
\frac{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}{3 - \cos^2\alpha - \cos^2\beta - \cos^2\gamma}
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
By the Law of Sines: $a = 2R\sin\alpha$, $b = 2R\sin\beta$, $c = 2R\sin\gamma$.

So:
\[
a^2 + b^2 + c^2 = 4R^2(\sin^2\alpha + \sin^2\beta + \sin^2\gamma)
\]

For the denominator, we use $\sin^2\theta = 1 - \cos^2\theta$:
\[
3 - \cos^2\alpha - \cos^2\beta - \cos^2\gamma = 3 - (1 - \sin^2\alpha) - (1 - \sin^2\beta) - (1 - \sin^2\gamma)
\]
\[
= 3 - 3 + \sin^2\alpha + \sin^2\beta + \sin^2\gamma = \sin^2\alpha + \sin^2\beta + \sin^2\gamma
\]

Therefore:
\[
\frac{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}{3 - \cos^2\alpha - \cos^2\beta - \cos^2\gamma} = \frac{4R^2(\sin^2\alpha + \sin^2\beta + \sin^2\gamma)}{\sin^2\alpha + \sin^2\beta + \sin^2\gamma} = 4R^2
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
\textbf{(D)} $4R^2$
