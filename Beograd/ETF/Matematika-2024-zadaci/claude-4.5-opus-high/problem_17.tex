\section*{Problem 17}
Let there be a triangle with sides $a$, $b$, $c$ and area $P = 15\sqrt{3}$ cm$^2$, 
circumradius $R = \frac{14\sqrt{3}}{3}$ cm. If $a$ is the side of length 10 cm 
opposite to an acute angle, find the sum of lengths of sides $b$ and $c$ (in cm).

\subsection*{Solution}
Using the formula $P = \frac{abc}{4R}$:
\[
15\sqrt{3} = \frac{10 \cdot bc}{4 \cdot \frac{14\sqrt{3}}{3}}
\]
\[
15\sqrt{3} = \frac{10 \cdot bc}{\frac{56\sqrt{3}}{3}} = \frac{30bc}{56\sqrt{3}}
\]
\[
15\sqrt{3} \cdot 56\sqrt{3} = 30bc
\]
\[
15 \cdot 56 \cdot 3 = 30bc
\]
\[
2520 = 30bc
\]
\[
bc = 84
\]

Using the formula $P = \frac{1}{2}bc \sin A$:
\[
15\sqrt{3} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 84 \cdot \sin A
\]
\[
\sin A = \frac{30\sqrt{3}}{84} = \frac{5\sqrt{3}}{14}
\]

Since $A$ is acute:
\[
\cos A = \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 A} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{75}{196}} = \sqrt{\frac{121}{196}} = \frac{11}{14}
\]

Using the Law of Cosines: $a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$
\[
100 = b^2 + c^2 - 2 \cdot 84 \cdot \frac{11}{14}
\]
\[
100 = b^2 + c^2 - 132
\]
\[
b^2 + c^2 = 232
\]

Now we can find $b + c$:
\[
(b + c)^2 = b^2 + 2bc + c^2 = 232 + 2 \cdot 84 = 232 + 168 = 400
\]
\[
b + c = 20
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$20$ (option \textbf{D}).
