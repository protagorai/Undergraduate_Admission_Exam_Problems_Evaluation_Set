\section*{Problem 7}
The base of a pyramid is a right triangle $ABC$ with hypotenuse $AB = c$ and angle $\angle BAC = 30°$. 
If $O$ is the midpoint of hypotenuse $AB$ and the orthogonal projection of pyramid vertex $S$ 
satisfies $SO = AO$, find the volume of the pyramid.

\subsection*{Solution}
In right triangle $ABC$ with right angle at $C$:
\begin{itemize}
\item Hypotenuse $AB = c$
\item $\angle BAC = 30°$, so $\angle ABC = 60°$
\item $BC = c \sin 30° = \frac{c}{2}$ (opposite to angle $A$)
\item $AC = c \cos 30° = \frac{c\sqrt{3}}{2}$ (adjacent to angle $A$)
\end{itemize}

Area of the base triangle:
\[
\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot BC \cdot AC = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{c}{2} \cdot \frac{c\sqrt{3}}{2} = \frac{c^2\sqrt{3}}{8}
\]

Since $O$ is the midpoint of $AB$:
\[
AO = \frac{c}{2}
\]

The height of the pyramid is $SO = AO = \frac{c}{2}$.

Volume of the pyramid:
\[
V = \frac{1}{3} \cdot \text{Area} \cdot h = \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{c^2\sqrt{3}}{8} \cdot \frac{c}{2} = \frac{c^3\sqrt{3}}{48}
\]

Note: $\displaystyle\frac{c^3\sqrt{3}}{48} = \frac{c^3}{48/\sqrt{3}} = \frac{c^3}{16\sqrt{3}}$

\subsection*{Answer}
$\displaystyle\frac{c^3}{16\sqrt{3}}$ (option \textbf{D}).
