\section*{Problem 13}
Find the product of all parameters $a$ for which the system
\[
\begin{cases}
x^2 + y^2 + 2x \leq 1 \\
x - y + a = 0
\end{cases}
\]
has a unique solution.

\subsection*{Solution}
First, rewrite the inequality as a circle:
\[
x^2 + y^2 + 2x \leq 1
\]
\[
(x+1)^2 - 1 + y^2 \leq 1
\]
\[
(x+1)^2 + y^2 \leq 2
\]

This is a closed disk with center $(-1, 0)$ and radius $\sqrt{2}$.

The line $x - y + a = 0$ can be written as $y = x + a$.

For the system to have a unique solution, the line must be tangent to the circle (touching the boundary at exactly one point, with no points inside).

Distance from center $(-1, 0)$ to line $x - y + a = 0$:
\[
d = \frac{|(-1) - 0 + a|}{\sqrt{1^2 + (-1)^2}} = \frac{|a - 1|}{\sqrt{2}}
\]

For tangency:
\[
d = \sqrt{2}
\]
\[
\frac{|a - 1|}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{2}
\]
\[
|a - 1| = 2
\]
\[
a - 1 = 2 \quad \text{or} \quad a - 1 = -2
\]
\[
a = 3 \quad \text{or} \quad a = -1
\]

Product of all values:
\[
3 \times (-1) = -3
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-3$ (option \textbf{C}).
