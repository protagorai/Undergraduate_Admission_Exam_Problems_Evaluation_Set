\section*{Problem 14}
Three points divide a circle in the ratio $6:7:11$. Find the value of the smallest angle of the triangle formed by these points.

\subsection*{Solution}
The three arcs are in ratio $6:7:11$.

Total parts: $6 + 7 + 11 = 24$

Each part corresponds to:
\[
\frac{360°}{24} = 15°
\]

The three arcs subtend central angles:
\begin{itemize}
\item Arc 1: $6 \times 15° = 90°$
\item Arc 2: $7 \times 15° = 105°$
\item Arc 3: $11 \times 15° = 165°$
\end{itemize}

By the inscribed angle theorem, an inscribed angle is half the central angle subtending the same arc.

Each angle of the triangle is inscribed and subtends the arc opposite to it:
\begin{itemize}
\item Angle opposite to arc of $90°$: $\frac{90°}{2} = 45°$
\item Angle opposite to arc of $105°$: $\frac{105°}{2} = 52.5°$
\item Angle opposite to arc of $165°$: $\frac{165°}{2} = 82.5°$
\end{itemize}

Verification: $45° + 52.5° + 82.5° = 180°$ \checkmark

The smallest angle is $45°$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$45°$ (option \textbf{D}).
