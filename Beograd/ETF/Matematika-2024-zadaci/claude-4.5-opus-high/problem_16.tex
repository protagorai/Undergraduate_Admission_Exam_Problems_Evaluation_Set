\section*{Problem 16}
Find the limit:
\[
\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^{\sin^2 x} - \cos x}{\sqrt{x^2 + 1} - 1}
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
We use Taylor series expansions around $x = 0$.

\textbf{Numerator:}

For small $x$: $\sin x \approx x$, so $\sin^2 x \approx x^2$.

\[
e^{\sin^2 x} \approx e^{x^2} \approx 1 + x^2 + \frac{x^4}{2} + \ldots \approx 1 + x^2
\]

\[
\cos x \approx 1 - \frac{x^2}{2} + \frac{x^4}{24} - \ldots \approx 1 - \frac{x^2}{2}
\]

Numerator:
\[
e^{\sin^2 x} - \cos x \approx (1 + x^2) - \left(1 - \frac{x^2}{2}\right) = x^2 + \frac{x^2}{2} = \frac{3x^2}{2}
\]

\textbf{Denominator:}

Using $\sqrt{1 + u} \approx 1 + \frac{u}{2}$ for small $u$:
\[
\sqrt{x^2 + 1} = \sqrt{1 + x^2} \approx 1 + \frac{x^2}{2}
\]

Denominator:
\[
\sqrt{x^2 + 1} - 1 \approx \frac{x^2}{2}
\]

\textbf{Limit:}
\[
\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{e^{\sin^2 x} - \cos x}{\sqrt{x^2 + 1} - 1} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{3x^2}{2}}{\frac{x^2}{2}} = \frac{3}{1} = 3
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$3$ (option \textbf{D}).
