\section*{Problem 18}
Find the maximum volume $V$ of a regular hexagonal pyramid inscribed in a sphere of radius $R$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $r$ be the radius of the base hexagon (distance from center to vertex) and $h$ be the height of the pyramid.

Set up coordinates with the sphere center at the origin. Let the base of the pyramid be at height $-y$ below the origin (where $y > 0$), and the apex at height $h - y$.

From the sphere equation:
\begin{itemize}
\item Base vertices: $r^2 + y^2 = R^2$
\item Apex: $(h - y)^2 = R^2$, so $h - y = R$ (taking positive), thus $y = h - R$
\end{itemize}

Substituting:
\[
r^2 + (h - R)^2 = R^2
\]
\[
r^2 = R^2 - (h - R)^2 = R^2 - h^2 + 2hR - R^2 = 2hR - h^2 = h(2R - h)
\]

\textbf{Volume of regular hexagonal pyramid:}

Base area of regular hexagon with circumradius $r$:
\[
A = \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}r^2 = \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}h(2R - h)
\]

Volume:
\[
V = \frac{1}{3}Ah = \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}h(2R - h) \cdot h = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}h^2(2R - h)
\]

\textbf{Maximizing:}
\[
\frac{dV}{dh} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\left[2h(2R - h) + h^2(-1)\right] = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}[4hR - 2h^2 - h^2] = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}h(4R - 3h)
\]

Setting $\frac{dV}{dh} = 0$: $h = 0$ or $h = \frac{4R}{3}$.

At $h = \frac{4R}{3}$:
\[
V = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\left(\frac{4R}{3}\right)^2\left(2R - \frac{4R}{3}\right) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{16R^2}{9} \cdot \frac{2R}{3} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{32R^3}{27} = \frac{16\sqrt{3}R^3}{27}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\displaystyle\frac{16\sqrt{3}R^3}{27}$ (option \textbf{A}).
