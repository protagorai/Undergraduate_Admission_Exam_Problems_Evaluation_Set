\section*{Problem 20}
Find the number of ways to choose four distinct numbers from the set $\{1, 2, \ldots, 9\}$ 
such that at least one of them is divisible by 4 and at least one is strictly greater than 5.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let:
\begin{itemize}
\item $A$ = selections with at least one number divisible by 4
\item $B$ = selections with at least one number $> 5$
\end{itemize}

Numbers divisible by 4 in $\{1, \ldots, 9\}$: $\{4, 8\}$

Numbers $> 5$ in $\{1, \ldots, 9\}$: $\{6, 7, 8, 9\}$

We want $|A \cap B|$.

Using inclusion-exclusion:
\[
|A \cap B| = |U| - |A^c| - |B^c| + |A^c \cap B^c|
\]

where $U$ = all 4-selections from $\{1, \ldots, 9\}$.

\textbf{Calculate each term:}

$|U| = \binom{9}{4} = 126$

$|A^c|$ = no number divisible by 4 = choose from $\{1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 9\}$ (7 elements)
\[
|A^c| = \binom{7}{4} = 35
\]

$|B^c|$ = no number $> 5$ = choose from $\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}$ (5 elements)
\[
|B^c| = \binom{5}{4} = 5
\]

$|A^c \cap B^c|$ = no number divisible by 4 AND no number $> 5$ = choose from $\{1, 2, 3, 5\}$ (4 elements)
\[
|A^c \cap B^c| = \binom{4}{4} = 1
\]

\textbf{Result:}
\[
|A \cap B| = 126 - 35 - 5 + 1 = 87
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$87$ (option \textbf{B}).
