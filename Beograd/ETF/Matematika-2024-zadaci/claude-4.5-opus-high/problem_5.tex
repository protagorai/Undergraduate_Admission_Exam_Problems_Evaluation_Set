\section*{Problem 5}
In the binomial expansion of $\displaystyle\left(2^x - \frac{3}{4^x}\right)^9$, $x \in \mathbb{R}\setminus\{0\}$, 
find the term that does not contain $x$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Using the binomial theorem, the general term is:
\[
T_{k+1} = \binom{9}{k} (2^x)^{9-k} \left(-\frac{3}{4^x}\right)^k
\]

Simplifying:
\[
T_{k+1} = \binom{9}{k} (-3)^k \cdot 2^{x(9-k)} \cdot 4^{-kx}
\]
\[
= \binom{9}{k} (-3)^k \cdot 2^{x(9-k)} \cdot 2^{-2kx}
\]
\[
= \binom{9}{k} (-3)^k \cdot 2^{x(9-k-2k)}
\]
\[
= \binom{9}{k} (-3)^k \cdot 2^{x(9-3k)}
\]

For the term to not contain $x$, we need:
\[
9 - 3k = 0 \implies k = 3
\]

The term is:
\[
T_4 = \binom{9}{3} (-3)^3 \cdot 2^0 = 84 \cdot (-27) \cdot 1 = -2268
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-2268$ (option \textbf{E}).
