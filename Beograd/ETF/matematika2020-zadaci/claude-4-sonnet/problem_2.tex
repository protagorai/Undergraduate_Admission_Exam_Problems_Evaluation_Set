\section*{Problem 2}
If the surface area of a circular sector is 15\% of the surface area of the circle, what is the angle of that circular sector?

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the radius of the circle be $r$ and the central angle of the sector be $\theta$ (in degrees).

The area of the full circle is $\pi r^2$.

The area of a sector with central angle $\theta$ degrees is:
\[
A_{sector} = \frac{\theta}{360°} \cdot \pi r^2
\]

According to the problem:
\[
A_{sector} = 0.15 \cdot A_{circle}
\]

Substituting:
\[
\frac{\theta}{360°} \cdot \pi r^2 = 0.15 \cdot \pi r^2
\]

Dividing both sides by $\pi r^2$:
\[
\frac{\theta}{360°} = 0.15
\]

Solving for $\theta$:
\[
\theta = 0.15 \times 360° = 54°
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$54°$ (option \textbf{D}).
