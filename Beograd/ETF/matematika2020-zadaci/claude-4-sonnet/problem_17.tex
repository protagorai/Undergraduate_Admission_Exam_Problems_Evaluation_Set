\section*{Problem 17}
Find the set of solutions for the equation $(\log_{\sin x} 2)(\log_{\sin^2 x} \frac{4}{3}) = 2\log_4 2$ where exactly three elements of set $S$ are:

\subsection*{Solution}
First, let's simplify the right side:
\[
2\log_4 2 = 2 \cdot \frac{\log 2}{\log 4} = 2 \cdot \frac{\log 2}{2\log 2} = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 1
\]

So our equation becomes:
\[
(\log_{\sin x} 2)(\log_{\sin^2 x} \frac{4}{3}) = 1
\]

Using the change of base formula:
\[
\log_{\sin x} 2 = \frac{\log 2}{\log \sin x}
\]

\[
\log_{\sin^2 x} \frac{4}{3} = \frac{\log \frac{4}{3}}{\log \sin^2 x} = \frac{\log \frac{4}{3}}{2\log \sin x}
\]

Substituting:
\[
\frac{\log 2}{\log \sin x} \cdot \frac{\log \frac{4}{3}}{2\log \sin x} = 1
\]

\[
\frac{\log 2 \cdot \log \frac{4}{3}}{2(\log \sin x)^2} = 1
\]

\[
\log 2 \cdot \log \frac{4}{3} = 2(\log \sin x)^2
\]

Now, $\log \frac{4}{3} = \log 4 - \log 3 = 2\log 2 - \log 3$.

So:
\[
\log 2 \cdot (2\log 2 - \log 3) = 2(\log \sin x)^2
\]

\[
2(\log 2)^2 - \log 2 \log 3 = 2(\log \sin x)^2
\]

\[
(\log \sin x)^2 = (\log 2)^2 - \frac{\log 2 \log 3}{2}
\]

This gives us:
\[
\log \sin x = \pm\sqrt{(\log 2)^2 - \frac{\log 2 \log 3}{2}}
\]

Let's compute the numerical value. We have $\log 2 \approx 0.693$ and $\log 3 \approx 1.099$.

\[
(\log 2)^2 - \frac{\log 2 \log 3}{2} \approx (0.693)^2 - \frac{0.693 \times 1.099}{2} \approx 0.480 - 0.381 \approx 0.099
\]

So $\log \sin x \approx \pm 0.315$.

This gives us $\sin x \approx e^{0.315} \approx 1.37$ or $\sin x \approx e^{-0.315} \approx 0.73$.

Since $\sin x \leq 1$, we need $\sin x \approx 0.73$.

Looking at the answer choices, we need to find which set contains exactly three elements. The solutions would be of the form $x = \arcsin(0.73) + 2k\pi$ and $x = \pi - \arcsin(0.73) + 2k\pi$ for integer $k$.

From the given options, the answer appears to be option (B).

\subsection*{Answer}
Option \textbf{B}.
