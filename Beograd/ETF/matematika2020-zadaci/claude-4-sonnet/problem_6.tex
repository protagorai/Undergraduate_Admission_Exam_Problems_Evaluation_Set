\section*{Problem 6}
Find the product of the smallest and largest roots of polynomial $P(x) = x^4 - 9x^2 + 18$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let's substitute $u = x^2$ to get a quadratic equation:
\[
P(x) = x^4 - 9x^2 + 18 = u^2 - 9u + 18
\]

Solving the quadratic $u^2 - 9u + 18 = 0$:
\[
u = \frac{9 \pm \sqrt{81 - 72}}{2} = \frac{9 \pm \sqrt{9}}{2} = \frac{9 \pm 3}{2}
\]

So: $u_1 = \frac{9 + 3}{2} = 6$ and $u_2 = \frac{9 - 3}{2} = 3$

Since $u = x^2$, we have:
\begin{align}
x^2 &= 6 \Rightarrow x = \pm\sqrt{6}\\
x^2 &= 3 \Rightarrow x = \pm\sqrt{3}
\end{align}

The four roots are: $-\sqrt{6}, -\sqrt{3}, \sqrt{3}, \sqrt{6}$

The smallest root is $-\sqrt{6}$ and the largest root is $\sqrt{6}$.

Their product is:
\[
(-\sqrt{6}) \cdot \sqrt{6} = -6
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-6$ (option \textbf{D}).
