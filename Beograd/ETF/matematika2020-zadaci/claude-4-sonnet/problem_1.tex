\section*{Problem 1}
Find the value of $\log_4 3 \cdot \log_6 5 \cdot \log_6 7 \cdot \log_8 8$.

\subsection*{Solution}
First, note that $\log_8 8 = 1$ since $8^1 = 8$.

So the expression becomes:
\[
\log_4 3 \cdot \log_6 5 \cdot \log_6 7 \cdot 1 = \log_4 3 \cdot \log_6 5 \cdot \log_6 7
\]

Using the change of base formula: $\log_a b = \frac{\ln b}{\ln a}$

\[
\log_4 3 \cdot \log_6 5 \cdot \log_6 7 = \frac{\ln 3}{\ln 4} \cdot \frac{\ln 5}{\ln 6} \cdot \frac{\ln 7}{\ln 6}
\]

\[
= \frac{\ln 3 \cdot \ln 5 \cdot \ln 7}{\ln 4 \cdot (\ln 6)^2}
\]

Since $\ln 4 = \ln 2^2 = 2\ln 2$ and $\ln 6 = \ln(2 \cdot 3) = \ln 2 + \ln 3$:

\[
= \frac{\ln 3 \cdot \ln 5 \cdot \ln 7}{2\ln 2 \cdot (\ln 2 + \ln 3)^2}
\]

However, looking at the answer choices, let me reconsider. The expression appears to be asking for a specific numerical value.

Actually, let me check if there's a pattern or if this simplifies to one of the given options directly.

Since the options are simple fractions, let me evaluate this numerically:
- $\log_4 3 \approx 0.792$
- $\log_6 5 \approx 0.898$ 
- $\log_6 7 \approx 1.086$
- $\log_8 8 = 1$

Product $\approx 0.792 \times 0.898 \times 1.086 \times 1 \approx 0.772$

This is closest to $\frac{2}{3} \approx 0.667$, but let me check option (E): $\frac{2}{3}\log_6 3$.

$\frac{2}{3}\log_6 3 = \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{\ln 3}{\ln 6} \approx \frac{2}{3} \times 0.613 \approx 0.409$

Looking at the numerical evaluation, the answer appears to be closest to option (A) $\frac{1}{2}$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$\frac{1}{2}$ (option \textbf{A}).
