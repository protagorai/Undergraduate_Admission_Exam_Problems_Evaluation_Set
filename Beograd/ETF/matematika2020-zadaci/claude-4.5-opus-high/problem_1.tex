\section*{Problem 1}
Find the value of the expression $\log_4 3 \cdot \log_5 4 \cdot \log_6 5 \cdot \log_7 6 \cdot \log_8 7 \cdot \log_9 8$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Using the change of base formula, we have $\log_a b = \frac{\ln b}{\ln a}$.

Therefore:
\[
\log_4 3 \cdot \log_5 4 \cdot \log_6 5 \cdot \log_7 6 \cdot \log_8 7 \cdot \log_9 8
\]
\[
= \frac{\ln 3}{\ln 4} \cdot \frac{\ln 4}{\ln 5} \cdot \frac{\ln 5}{\ln 6} \cdot \frac{\ln 6}{\ln 7} \cdot \frac{\ln 7}{\ln 8} \cdot \frac{\ln 8}{\ln 9}
\]

This is a telescoping product where consecutive terms cancel:
\[
= \frac{\ln 3}{\ln 9} = \frac{\ln 3}{\ln 3^2} = \frac{\ln 3}{2\ln 3} = \frac{1}{2}
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$\frac{1}{2}$ (option \textbf{A}).
