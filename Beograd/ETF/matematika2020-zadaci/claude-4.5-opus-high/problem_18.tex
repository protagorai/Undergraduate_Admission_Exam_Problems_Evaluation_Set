\section*{Problem 18}
In a cyclic quadrilateral $ABCD$, diagonal $BD$ is perpendicular to side $BC$, angles $\angle ABC$ and $\angle BAD$ are both equal to $120°$, and the length of side $AD$ is 1 cm. Find the product of the length of diagonal $BD$ and the length of side $CD$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Given information:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $ABCD$ is a cyclic quadrilateral (inscribed in a circle)
    \item $BD \perp BC$
    \item $\angle ABC = \angle BAD = 120°$
    \item $AD = 1$ cm
\end{itemize}

In a cyclic quadrilateral, opposite angles sum to $180°$:
\[
\angle ABC + \angle ADC = 180° \implies \angle ADC = 180° - 120° = 60°
\]
\[
\angle BAD + \angle BCD = 180° \implies \angle BCD = 180° - 120° = 60°
\]

Since $BD \perp BC$, we have $\angle DBC = 90°$.

In triangle $BCD$:
\[
\angle BCD + \angle BDC + \angle DBC = 180°
\]
\[
60° + \angle BDC + 90° = 180° \implies \angle BDC = 30°
\]

In triangle $ABD$:
\[
\angle ABD = \angle ABC - \angle DBC = 120° - 90° = 30°
\]
\[
\angle ADB = 180° - \angle BAD - \angle ABD = 180° - 120° - 30° = 30°
\]

Since $\angle ABD = \angle ADB = 30°$, triangle $ABD$ is isoceles with $AD = AB = 1$ cm.

Using the Law of Sines in triangle $ABD$:
\[
\frac{BD}{\sin(\angle BAD)} = \frac{AD}{\sin(\angle ABD)}
\]
\[
\frac{BD}{\sin 120°} = \frac{1}{\sin 30°}
\]
\[
BD = \frac{\sin 120°}{\sin 30°} = \frac{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{3} \text{ cm}
\]

Using the Law of Sines in triangle $BCD$:
\[
\frac{CD}{\sin(\angle DBC)} = \frac{BD}{\sin(\angle BCD)}
\]
\[
\frac{CD}{\sin 90°} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sin 60°}
\]
\[
CD = \frac{\sqrt{3} \cdot 1}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{3} \cdot 2}{\sqrt{3}} = 2 \text{ cm}
\]

The product $BD \cdot CD = \sqrt{3} \cdot 2 = 2\sqrt{3}$ cm$^2$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2\sqrt{3}$ cm$^2$ (option \textbf{C}).
