\section*{Problem 2}
If the area of a circular sector is 15\% of the area of the circle, what is the central angle of that sector?

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $r$ be the radius of the circle and $\theta$ be the central angle (in degrees).

The area of the full circle is $\pi r^2$.

The area of a sector with central angle $\theta$ degrees is:
\[
A_{\text{sector}} = \frac{\theta}{360°} \cdot \pi r^2
\]

Given that the sector area is 15\% of the circle area:
\[
\frac{\theta}{360°} \cdot \pi r^2 = 0.15 \cdot \pi r^2
\]

Dividing both sides by $\pi r^2$:
\[
\frac{\theta}{360°} = 0.15 = \frac{15}{100} = \frac{3}{20}
\]

Therefore:
\[
\theta = 360° \cdot \frac{3}{20} = \frac{1080°}{20} = 54°
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$54°$ (option \textbf{D}).
