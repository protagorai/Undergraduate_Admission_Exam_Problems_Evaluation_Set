\section*{Problem 6}
Find the product of the smallest and largest roots of the polynomial $P(x) = x^4 - 9x^2 + 18$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let $y = x^2$. Then the equation becomes:
\[
y^2 - 9y + 18 = 0
\]

Using the quadratic formula:
\[
y = \frac{9 \pm \sqrt{81 - 72}}{2} = \frac{9 \pm \sqrt{9}}{2} = \frac{9 \pm 3}{2}
\]

So $y_1 = \frac{9 + 3}{2} = 6$ and $y_2 = \frac{9 - 3}{2} = 3$.

Since $y = x^2$:
\begin{itemize}
    \item From $x^2 = 6$: $x = \pm\sqrt{6}$
    \item From $x^2 = 3$: $x = \pm\sqrt{3}$
\end{itemize}

The four roots are: $-\sqrt{6}, -\sqrt{3}, \sqrt{3}, \sqrt{6}$.

The smallest root is $-\sqrt{6}$ and the largest root is $\sqrt{6}$.

Their product is:
\[
(-\sqrt{6}) \cdot \sqrt{6} = -6
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-6$ (option \textbf{D}).
