\section*{Problem 8 - Solution}

\textbf{Problem:} Find the number of solutions of $(\sin 2x - \cos 2x)^2 = \sin 4x$ in the interval $\left[0, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$.

\textbf{Solution:}

Expand the left side:
\[
(\sin 2x - \cos 2x)^2 = \sin^2 2x - 2\sin 2x \cos 2x + \cos^2 2x
\]

Using $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$ and $2\sin\theta\cos\theta = \sin 2\theta$:
\[
= 1 - 2\sin 2x \cos 2x = 1 - \sin 4x
\]

So the equation becomes:
\[
1 - \sin 4x = \sin 4x
\]
\[
1 = 2\sin 4x
\]
\[
\sin 4x = \frac{1}{2}
\]

For $x \in \left[0, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$, we have $4x \in [0, 2\pi]$.

Solutions of $\sin\theta = \frac{1}{2}$ in $[0, 2\pi]$:
\[
\theta = \frac{\pi}{6} \quad \text{or} \quad \theta = \frac{5\pi}{6}
\]

Therefore:
\[
4x = \frac{\pi}{6} \implies x = \frac{\pi}{24}
\]
\[
4x = \frac{5\pi}{6} \implies x = \frac{5\pi}{24}
\]

Both solutions are in $\left[0, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]$ since $\frac{5\pi}{24} < \frac{12\pi}{24} = \frac{\pi}{2}$.

\textbf{Number of solutions: 2}

\textbf{Answer: Ð’) 2}
