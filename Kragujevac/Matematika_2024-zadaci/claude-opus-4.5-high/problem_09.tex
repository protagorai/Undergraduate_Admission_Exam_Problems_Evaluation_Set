\section*{Problem 9 - Solution}

\textbf{Problem:} Find the side length of a rhombus with area $24\,\text{cm}^2$ whose diagonals are in ratio $3:4$.

\textbf{Solution:}

Let the diagonals be $d_1 = 3k$ and $d_2 = 4k$ for some positive $k$.

The area of a rhombus is:
\[
A = \frac{1}{2}d_1 \cdot d_2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3k \cdot 4k = 6k^2
\]

Given $A = 24\,\text{cm}^2$:
\[
6k^2 = 24
\]
\[
k^2 = 4
\]
\[
k = 2
\]

So the diagonals are:
\begin{itemize}
\item $d_1 = 3 \cdot 2 = 6\,\text{cm}$
\item $d_2 = 4 \cdot 2 = 8\,\text{cm}$
\end{itemize}

The diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles. The half-diagonals are $3\,\text{cm}$ and $4\,\text{cm}$.

Using the Pythagorean theorem, the side of the rhombus is:
\[
a = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5\,\text{cm}
\]

\textbf{Answer: Ð‘) 5 cm}
