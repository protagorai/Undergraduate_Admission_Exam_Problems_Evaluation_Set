\section*{Problem 3 - Solution}

\textbf{Problem:} Find the number of integer solutions of the inequality:
\[
\frac{x-2}{x^2+3x-4} > \frac{1}{3}
\]

\textbf{Solution:}

First, factor the denominator: $x^2 + 3x - 4 = (x+4)(x-1)$

Rewrite the inequality:
\[
\frac{x-2}{(x+4)(x-1)} - \frac{1}{3} > 0
\]

\[
\frac{3(x-2) - (x+4)(x-1)}{3(x+4)(x-1)} > 0
\]

Expand the numerator:
\begin{align*}
3(x-2) - (x+4)(x-1) &= 3x - 6 - (x^2 + 3x - 4) \\
&= 3x - 6 - x^2 - 3x + 4 \\
&= -x^2 - 2
\end{align*}

So we have:
\[
\frac{-x^2 - 2}{3(x+4)(x-1)} > 0
\]

Since $-x^2 - 2 < 0$ for all real $x$ (always negative), and $3 > 0$, we need:
\[
(x+4)(x-1) < 0
\]

This inequality holds when $-4 < x < 1$.

Domain restrictions: $x \neq -4$ and $x \neq 1$

Integer solutions in $(-4, 1)$: $\{-3, -2, -1, 0\}$

\textbf{Number of integer solutions: 4}
