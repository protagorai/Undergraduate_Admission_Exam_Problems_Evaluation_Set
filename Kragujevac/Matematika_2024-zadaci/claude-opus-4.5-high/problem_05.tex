\section*{Problem 5 - Solution}

\textbf{Problem:} Find the sum of all real solutions of the equation $(x+3)\sqrt{12-|x|} = 0$.

\textbf{Solution:}

For the product to equal zero, either factor must be zero:

\textbf{Case 1:} $x + 3 = 0$
\[
x = -3
\]
Check domain: $12 - |-3| = 12 - 3 = 9 \geq 0$ \checkmark

\textbf{Case 2:} $\sqrt{12-|x|} = 0$
\[
12 - |x| = 0 \implies |x| = 12 \implies x = 12 \text{ or } x = -12
\]
Both satisfy the domain requirement (equality case).

All solutions: $x \in \{-12, -3, 12\}$

\textbf{Sum of solutions:}
\[
(-12) + (-3) + 12 = -3
\]

\textbf{Answer: Ð‘) $-3$}
