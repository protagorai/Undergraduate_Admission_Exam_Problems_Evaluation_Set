\section*{Problem 3}
Find the number of integer solutions of
\[
\frac{x-2}{x^2+3x-4}>\frac13.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Factor the denominator:
\[
x^2+3x-4=(x+4)(x-1),\qquad x\ne -4,1.
\]
Bring to one side:
\[
\frac{x-2}{(x+4)(x-1)}-\frac13>0
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
\frac{3(x-2)-(x+4)(x-1)}{3(x+4)(x-1)}>0.
\]
Compute the numerator:
\[
3(x-2)-(x+4)(x-1)=3x-6-(x^2+3x-4)=-x^2-2=-(x^2+2).
\]
Hence
\[
\frac{-(x^2+2)}{3(x+4)(x-1)}>0
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
\frac{x^2+2}{(x+4)(x-1)}<0.
\]
Since $x^2+2>0$ for all $x$, we need
\[
(x+4)(x-1)<0 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad -4<x<1.
\]
Integer $x$ in $(-4,1)$ are $-3,-2,-1,0$, so there are $4$ solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$4$ (option \textbf{Ð“}).
