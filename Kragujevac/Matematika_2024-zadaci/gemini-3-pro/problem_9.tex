\section*{Problem 9}
A rhombus has area $24\text{ cm}^2$ and the ratio of its diagonals is $3:4$.
Find the length of its side.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let diagonals be $d_1=3k$ and $d_2=4k$. Area of a rhombus:
\[
S=\frac{d_1d_2}{2}=\frac{(3k)(4k)}{2}=6k^2.
\]
Given $S=24$, we get $6k^2=24\Rightarrow k^2=4\Rightarrow k=2$.
So $d_1=6$ and $d_2=8$.

The side is the hypotenuse formed by half-diagonals:
\[
s=\sqrt{\left(\frac{d_1}{2}\right)^2+\left(\frac{d_2}{2}\right)^2}
=\sqrt{3^2+4^2}=5.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$5\text{ cm}$ (option \textbf{Ð‘}).
