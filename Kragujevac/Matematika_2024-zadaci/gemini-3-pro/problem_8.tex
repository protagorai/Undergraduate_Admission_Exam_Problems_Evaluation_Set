\section*{Problem 8}
Find the number of solutions of
\[
(\sin 2x-\cos 2x)^2=\sin 4x
\]
that belong to the interval $\left[0,\frac{\pi}{2}\right]$.

\subsection*{Solution}
Expand:
\[
(\sin 2x-\cos 2x)^2=\sin^2 2x-2\sin 2x\cos 2x+\cos^2 2x
=1-\sin 4x,
\]
since $2\sin 2x\cos 2x=\sin 4x$.
Thus the equation becomes
\[
1-\sin 4x=\sin 4x \Rightarrow \sin 4x=\frac12.
\]
Let $\theta=4x$. For $x\in\left[0,\frac{\pi}{2}\right]$ we have $\theta\in[0,2\pi]$.
In $[0,2\pi]$, $\sin\theta=\frac12$ has solutions $\theta=\frac{\pi}{6},\frac{5\pi}{6}$.
Therefore
\[
x=\frac{\theta}{4}\in\left\{\frac{\pi}{24},\frac{5\pi}{24}\right\},
\]
so there are $2$ solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{Ð’}).
