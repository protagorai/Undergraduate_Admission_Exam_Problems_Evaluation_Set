\section*{Problem 11}
Lines
\[
p:\;2x+ay-15=0,\qquad q:\;x+2y-b-5=0
\]
intersect at the center of the circle
\[
k:\;x^2+y^2-10y+21=0.
\]
Which of the given lines contains the point $M(a,b)$?

\subsection*{Solution}
Complete the square for the circle:
\[
x^2+y^2-10y+21=0
\Rightarrow x^2+(y-5)^2-25+21=0
\Rightarrow x^2+(y-5)^2=4.
\]
So its center is $C=(0,5)$. Since $p$ and $q$ intersect at $C$, substitute $(0,5)$.

From $p$:
\[
2\cdot 0 + a\cdot 5 -15=0 \Rightarrow 5a=15 \Rightarrow a=3.
\]
From $q$:
\[
0+2\cdot 5 - b -5=0 \Rightarrow 5-b=0 \Rightarrow b=5.
\]
Thus $M(a,b)=(3,5)$.

Check which offered line passes through $(3,5)$. For
\[
2x+y-11=0:\quad 2\cdot 3+5-11=0,
\]
so it contains $M$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2x+y-11=0$ (option \textbf{Ð”}).
