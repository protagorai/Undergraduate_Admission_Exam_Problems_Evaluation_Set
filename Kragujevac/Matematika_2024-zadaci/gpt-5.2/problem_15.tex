\section*{Problem 15}
An increasing geometric sequence has the sum of its first three terms equal to $26$,
and the sum of its third, fourth, and fifth terms equal to $234$.
Find the product of the first and the fifth term.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let the terms be
\[
a,\; ar,\; ar^2,\; ar^3,\; ar^4,\qquad r>1.
\]
Then
\[
a(1+r+r^2)=26 \qquad (1)
\]
and
\[
ar^2(1+r+r^2)=234 \qquad (2)
\]
Divide (2) by (1):
\[
r^2=\frac{234}{26}=9 \Rightarrow r=3 \quad (r>1).
\]
From (1),
\[
a(1+3+9)=13a=26 \Rightarrow a=2.
\]
The fifth term is $ar^4=2\cdot 3^4=2\cdot 81=162$, so the required product is
\[
a\cdot ar^4=2\cdot 162=324.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$324$ (option \textbf{A}).
