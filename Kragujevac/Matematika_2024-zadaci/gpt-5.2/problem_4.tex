\section*{Problem 4}
Let $P(x)=ax^2+bx+c$. If $P(1)=1$, $P(0)=2$, $P(-1)=7$, compute $a^2+b^2+c^2$.

\subsection*{Solution}
From $P(0)=2$ we get $c=2$.

Then
\[
P(1)=a+b+c=1 \Rightarrow a+b+2=1 \Rightarrow a+b=-1,
\]
\[
P(-1)=a-b+c=7 \Rightarrow a-b+2=7 \Rightarrow a-b=5.
\]
Solving,
\[
2a=(a+b)+(a-b)=-1+5=4 \Rightarrow a=2,
\]
so $b=-3$ and $c=2$.
Therefore
\[
a^2+b^2+c^2=2^2+(-3)^2+2^2=4+9+4=17.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$17$ (option \textbf{Ð“}).
