\section*{Problem 8}
How many solutions of
\[
(\sin 2x-\cos 2x)^2=\sin 4x
\]
belong to the interval $\left[0,\frac{\pi}{2}\right]$?

\subsection*{Solution}
Expand:
\[
(\sin 2x-\cos 2x)^2=\sin^2 2x-2\sin 2x\cos 2x+\cos^2 2x
=1-\sin 4x.
\]
So the equation is
\[
1-\sin 4x=\sin 4x \Rightarrow \sin 4x=\frac12.
\]
With $x\in\left[0,\frac{\pi}{2}\right]$, we have $4x\in[0,2\pi]$.
In $[0,2\pi]$, $\sin \theta=\tfrac12$ at $\theta=\frac{\pi}{6}$ and $\theta=\frac{5\pi}{6}$.
Thus
\[
x\in\left\{\frac{\pi}{24},\frac{5\pi}{24}\right\},
\]
which gives $2$ solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
$2$ (option \textbf{Ð’}).
