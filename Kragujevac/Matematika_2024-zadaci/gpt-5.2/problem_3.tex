\section*{Problem 3}
Find the number of integer solutions of
\[
\frac{x-2}{x^2+3x-4}>\frac13.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Factor the denominator:
\[
x^2+3x-4=(x+4)(x-1),\qquad x\ne -4,1.
\]
Bring to one side:
\[
\frac{x-2}{(x+4)(x-1)}-\frac13>0
\Rightarrow
\frac{3(x-2)-(x+4)(x-1)}{3(x+4)(x-1)}>0.
\]
Compute the numerator:
\[
3(x-2)-(x+4)(x-1)=3x-6-(x^2+3x-4)=-x^2-2=-(x^2+2)<0.
\]
So the fraction is positive iff the denominator is negative:
\[
(x+4)(x-1)<0 \;\Rightarrow\; -4<x<1.
\]
Integers in this interval are $x\in\{-3,-2,-1,0\}$, giving $4$ solutions.

\subsection*{Answer}
