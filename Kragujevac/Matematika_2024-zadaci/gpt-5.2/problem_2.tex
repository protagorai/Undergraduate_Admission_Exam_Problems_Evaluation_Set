\section*{Problem 2}
Find the sum of all integer solutions of
\[
3|x-1|+x\le 4.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
Split by $x\ge 1$ and $x<1$.

\paragraph{Case 1: $x\ge 1$.}
Then $|x-1|=x-1$ and
\[
3(x-1)+x\le 4 \;\Rightarrow\; 4x-3\le 4 \;\Rightarrow\; x\le \tfrac{7}{4}.
\]
With $x\in\mathbb Z$ and $x\ge 1$, we get $x=1$.

\paragraph{Case 2: $x<1$.}
Then $|x-1|=1-x$ and
\[
3(1-x)+x\le 4 \;\Rightarrow\; 3-2x\le 4 \;\Rightarrow\; x\ge -\tfrac12.
\]
With $x\in\mathbb Z$ and $x<1$, we get $x=0$.

Thus the integer solutions are $\{0,1\}$ and their sum is $0+1=1$.

\subsection*{Answer}
$1$ (option \textbf{Ð‘}).
