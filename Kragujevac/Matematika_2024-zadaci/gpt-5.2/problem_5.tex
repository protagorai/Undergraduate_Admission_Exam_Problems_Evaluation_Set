\section*{Problem 5}
Find the sum of all real solutions of
\[
(x+3)\sqrt{12-|x|}=0.
\]

\subsection*{Solution}
The square root requires $12-|x|\ge 0$, i.e. $|x|\le 12$.

The product is zero iff
\[
x+3=0 \quad\text{or}\quad \sqrt{12-|x|}=0.
\]
Thus one solution is $x=-3$ (which satisfies $|x|\le 12$).

Also,
\[
\sqrt{12-|x|}=0 \Rightarrow 12-|x|=0 \Rightarrow |x|=12 \Rightarrow x=\pm 12.
\]
So the solutions are $-12,-3,12$, and their sum is
\[
(-12)+(-3)+12=-3.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$-3$ (option \textbf{Ð‘}).
