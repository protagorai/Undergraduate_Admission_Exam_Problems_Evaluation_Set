\section*{Problem 9}
A rhombus has area $24\text{ cm}^2$ and its diagonals are in ratio $3:4$.
Find the side length.

\subsection*{Solution}
Let diagonals be $d_1=3k$ and $d_2=4k$.
Then the area is
\[
S=\frac{d_1d_2}{2}=\frac{(3k)(4k)}{2}=6k^2=24 \Rightarrow k^2=4 \Rightarrow k=2.
\]
So $d_1=6$ and $d_2=8$.

In a rhombus, diagonals are perpendicular bisectors, so the side length is
\[
a=\sqrt{\left(\frac{d_1}{2}\right)^2+\left(\frac{d_2}{2}\right)^2}
\;=\;\sqrt{3^2+4^2}=\sqrt{25}=5.
\]

\subsection*{Answer}
$5\text{ cm}$ (option \textbf{Ð‘}).
